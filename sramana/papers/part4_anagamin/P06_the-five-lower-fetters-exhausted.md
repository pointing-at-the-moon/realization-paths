# The Five Lower Fetters Exhausted

**Why Non-Return Is a Change of Kind, Not One More Grade**

*中文主題：五下分結盡——不還何以是質變而非又一品*

## Abstract

The previous paper established the once-returner's "thinning" as a by-grade reduction — six of the desire-realm's nine grades of greed-and-hatred severed. Following this subtraction, the non-returner (anāgāmin) seems merely the quantitative continuation of "severing the remaining lower three grades." The thesis: although by count the non-returner is the continuation of "severing the seventh through ninth grade," his fruit-mark is a *change of kind* — the five lower fetters wholly severed, "not returning to this world." Quantity accumulated to a critical point becomes quality: once the fetters that bind to the desire-realm (chiefly sensual greed and ill-will) are wholly severed, the binding-force to the desire-realm falls to zero, and rebirth leaps from "a fixed number" (seven returns, one return) to "no return to this realm." The *Kośa* states it: "if he severs the ninth grade he becomes the non-return fruit, because he must not return to be reborn in the desire-realm," and names it "the five lower fetters severed" (a collective designation). The paper further clarifies: the non-returner's "non-return," the stream-enterer's "not falling," and the once-returner's "one return" are three rebirth-level projections of one subtraction, but "non-return" is the only one that is a *crossing of a realm* — the first two are still bound within the desire-realm, while the non-returner permanently leaves it, reborn in the Pure Abodes of the form-realm. Scripture depicts it with the "water simile": having crossed, one reaches the far shore; the sixth water-simile person is the non-returner, who has crossed the desire-realm's current and gained the far shore. Conclusion: non-return is not the quantitative increase of "one more grade severed" but the qualitative change of "desire-realm binding fallen to zero"; subtraction accumulated to this point breaks through, for the first time, the wall of a "realm."

**Keywords**: anāgāmin, non-returner, five lower fetters, sensual greed, ill-will, no return to the desire-realm, qualitative change, higher mental training, water simile, Pure Abodes, Abhidharmakośa, subtraction

## I. The Question

Following the previous paper's subtraction, the non-returner looks like a foregone entry in the ledger: the once-returner has severed six of the desire-realm's nine grades of greed-and-hatred; the non-returner merely severs the lower three more (the seventh through ninth) — by the numbers, six becomes nine, the continuation of three grades. If subtraction were only a count, this paper would have little to say: three more lamps put out.

But here scripture says something the first three fruits never said: "not returning to this world." The stream-enterer "does not fall to lower destinies" and the once-returner "returns once" — their rebirths, though of a fixed number, are still rebirths *within the desire-realm*; the non-returner "does not return to this world," "does not return to the desire-realm" — he is no longer reborn in the desire-realm at all, but ascends to the Pure Abodes of the form-realm and there attains final nibbāna. From "reborn in the desire-realm a certain number of times" to "never again reborn in the desire-realm" is not "a few times fewer," but a *crossing of the realm in which one is reborn*.

The thesis: the non-returner is the first *change of kind* subtraction produces. By count he is indeed the once-returner's continuation (severing the lower three grades), but the grades he severs are precisely the last remnant of desire-realm binding; the remnant exhausted, the force "binding to the desire-realm" falls to zero, so quantity accumulated to a critical point becomes quality — rebirth is no longer "a finite number within the desire-realm" but "outside the desire-realm." Subtraction here, for the first time, takes down not a brick within a wall but the wall of the "realm" itself.

The distinction matters because it reveals subtraction's non-linearity: the same "sever one grade," at the sixth grade (making a once-returner), only reduces rebirth to one return (still in the desire-realm); at the ninth grade (making a non-returner), it transforms rebirth into non-return to the desire-realm. The severance of the *last* grade crosses a threshold. The paper argues accordingly: Section II presents the basis (the listing of the five lower fetters, the scriptural proof of non-return, the treatise's definition of severing the ninth grade); Section III, the core argument, establishes in three layers that non-return is a change of kind (the critical point of desire-realm binding falling to zero; non-return's uniqueness among the three rebirth-projections; the water simile's crossing to the far shore); Section IV omits convergence; Section V offers corrections; Section VI concludes.

## II. The Canonical Basis

### 2.1 The five lower fetters listed; severed, one is "cool"

The five items of the five lower fetters the Āgamas list repeatedly. *Saṃyukta-āgama*, fascicle 18 (Jambukhādaka questioning Sāriputta), glosses them by "coolness":

> 清涼者，五下分結盡，謂身見、戒取、疑、貪欲、瞋恚。〔復問斷之之道，答：〕有。謂八正道——正見乃至正定。[^1]
>
> *"As to 'coolness' — the five lower fetters exhausted, namely identity-view, rite-grasping, doubt, sensual greed, ill-will. [Asked the path to sever them, he answered:] There is one — the eightfold noble path, from right view to right concentration."*

The five lower fetters = identity-view, rite-grasping, doubt, sensual greed, ill-will; exhaust these five, and it is called "gaining coolness"; the path to sever them is the eightfold path. The name "coolness" is telling — it depicts not "the gaining of some state" but the *extinguishing* of the desire-realm's fever (greed and hatred its flame), still the language of subtraction (less burning, hence cool), not of accumulation (some cool experience added).

### 2.2 No return to the desire-realm: the five lower exhausted, one does not return to this world

The non-returner's fruit-mark scripture marks directly with "not returning to this world." *Saṃyukta-āgama*, fascicle 29 (higher mental training), takes the concentration-training (higher mental training) among the three trainings as the cause of severing the five lower fetters:

> 如是知、如是見，斷於五下分結，謂身見、戒取、疑、貪欲、瞋恚。斷此五下分結，受生般涅槃，阿那含，不還此世，是名增上意學。[^2]
>
> *"Knowing thus, seeing thus, one severs the five lower fetters — identity-view, rite-grasping, doubt, sensual greed, ill-will. Having severed these five lower fetters, one takes [form-realm] birth and attains final nibbāna — a non-returner, not returning to this world. This is called the higher mental training."*

"Takes birth and attains final nibbāna… not returning to this world" — one who severs the five lower fetters takes birth in (the form-realm) and attains final nibbāna, no longer reborn in this (desire-)realm; this is the non-returner. The same sense in *Madhyama-āgama*, fascicle 1 (the Water-Simile sūtra), with the true knowledge-and-vision of the four noble truths as cause:

> 五下分結盡，謂貪欲、瞋恚、身見、戒取、疑，五下分結盡已，生於彼間，便般涅槃，得不退法，不還此世。[^3]
>
> *"The five lower fetters exhausted — sensual greed, ill-will, identity-view, rite-grasping, doubt — the five lower fetters being exhausted, one is born in that place [the form-realm] and there attains final nibbāna, gains the irreversible state, does not return to this world."*

"Born in that place, attains final nibbāna… does not return to this world" — born in that place (the form-realm) and there attaining final nibbāna, not returning to the desire-realm. Both sūtras mark the non-returner's core fruit-mark: no return to the desire-realm. Note the difference between this "non-return" and the stream-enterer's "not falling": "not falling" is not falling to the *bad destinies* (still reborn in the desire-realm's good destinies); "non-return" is not returning to the *desire-realm* (not reborn even in the desire-realm's good destinies). The one-character difference is the difference between within-the-realm and beyond-the-realm.

### 2.3 The treatise's definition: severing the ninth grade makes a non-returner, who must not be reborn in the desire-realm

The treatises pin this change of kind precisely to "severing the ninth grade." *Abhidharmakośa*, fascicle 24:

> 若斷第九成不還果，必不還來生欲界故。此或名曰五下結斷，雖必先斷或二或三，然於此時總集斷故。[^4]
>
> *"If he severs the ninth grade, he becomes the non-return fruit, because he must not return to be reborn in the desire-realm. This is also named 'the five lower fetters severed' — for although two or three must have been severed earlier, at this point they are severed as a collected whole."*

Two senses: first, "severs the ninth [grade], becomes the non-return fruit, must not return to be reborn in the desire-realm" — severing the ninth grade of desire-realm cultivation-defilement (the last grade) makes a non-returner, whose necessary fruit-mark is "not reborn in the desire-realm." Second, "this is also named 'the five lower fetters severed'… at this point severed as a collected whole" — the non-returner is also named "the five lower fetters severed"; though the first three fetters (identity-view, rite-grasping, doubt) were severed back at the path of seeing, and sensual greed and ill-will severed grade by grade, at the moment of severing the ninth grade the five lower fetters are *collectively* exhausted — hence "the five lower fetters exhausted" as the collective name of this position. The treatise's "severing the ninth grade" and scripture's "no return to the desire-realm" are one mechanism and one mark, pointing to one fruit.

> ⚠️ A note on register: the listing of the five lower fetters, "not returning to this world," and "coolness" (2.1–2.2) are stated outright in the Āgamas; "severing the ninth grade makes a non-returner" and "the five lower severed as a collected whole" (2.3) are the *Kośa*'s fine teaching on the path of cultivation, consonant with scripture but finer; drawn on here with its register as treatise marked.

## III. The Core Argument

### 3.1 The critical point of desire-realm binding falling to zero: the last grade severed crosses the wall of the realm

Why do severing the ninth grade and severing the sixth differ so vastly in effect? Because "severing the ninth grade" is precisely the moment when the desire-realm-binding fetters are *wholly* exhausted.

The principle of binding: a being is reborn in a given realm by being bound to that realm by fetters (greed and so on). The desire-realm's binding lies chiefly in sensual greed and ill-will (the latter two of the five lower fetters, and the prime summoners of desire-realm rebirth). The once-returner, severed through the sixth grade, still has the lower three grades of sensual greed and ill-will remaining — weak though the remnant is, it is still a true fetter, still with the force of binding to the desire-realm and summoning rebirth, hence still requiring "one return" in the desire-realm. Only with the ninth grade severed are the lower three grades also exhausted, sensual greed and ill-will *wholly* severed, the five lower fetters *collectively* exhausted — and at this point the force binding to the desire-realm falls to zero. The force at zero, "rebirth in the desire-realm" loses its cause (as P04 showed: rebirth requires a binding fetter as cause), so "no return to the desire-realm" is not forbidden but unable to occur.

This is the mechanism of the change of kind: the severance of the first eight grades is a *reduction* of desire-realm binding (rebirth decreasing: seven returns → one return); the severance of the ninth grade is the *zeroing* of desire-realm binding (rebirth transformed: non-return). Quantitative change (decreasing grade by grade) accumulated to the last grade touches the critical point and becomes qualitative change (crossing the realm). Subtraction here shows its non-linearity: not that each grade severed equally subtracts one rebirth, but that the last grade severed *triggers* the crossing of the "realm." This also echoes P05's lamp-simile and goes one step further: the nine lamps extinguished down to the sixth, the room is still in the desire-realm's dark (still one return required); extinguished to the ninth, the desire-realm's dark is *wholly* out, and the person leaves this dark room (no return to the desire-realm) — the extinguishing of the last lamp is not merely one shade less dark, but exiting the room.

### 3.2 Non-return's uniqueness among the three rebirth-projections: the only crossing of a realm

The stream-enterer's "not falling," the once-returner's "one return," and the non-returner's "non-return" are three rebirth-level projections of one subtraction (established grade by grade in P03 §3.3, P04 §3.3, P05 §3.3). But of the three, only the non-returner's "non-return" is a *crossing of a realm*; the first two are still within the desire-realm.

To distinguish: the stream-enterer's "not falling to lower destinies" — what is sealed is the desire-realm's *bad destinies* (hells, hungry ghosts, animals); he is still reborn in the desire-realm's *good destinies* (humans and gods, at most seven returns). The once-returner's "one return" — what is reduced is the *number* of desire-realm rebirths (down to one return); he is still reborn in the desire-realm. Both move within the rebirth-structure "inside the desire-realm" (rising in destiny, fewer in number). The non-returner's "not returning to this world" — what is crossed is the *desire-realm itself*: he is no longer reborn in any destiny of the desire-realm (not even the good destinies of humans and gods), but ascends to the Pure Abodes of the form-realm. The first two are optimizations within the realm; the non-returner is a change of kind out of the realm.

Thus the three projections, though sharing the source of subtraction, divide into "magnitude-level" and "kind-level": not-falling and one-return are magnitude-level (rising in destiny and decreasing in number, within the realm), non-return is kind-level (crossing the realm). This corresponds exactly to the critical-point thesis of 3.1 — subtraction decreasing grade by grade (quantity), at the collective exhaustion of the five lower fetters triggering the crossing of the realm (quality). The non-returner's uniqueness lies not in "severing the most" (the five higher fetters still remain) but in "crossing, for the first time, out of a realm."

> ✅ Doctrinally sound: the *Kośa*'s "severs the ninth, becomes the non-return fruit, must not return to be reborn in the desire-realm" establishes non-return = the fruit of the collective severance of desire-realm binding; contrasted with the stream-enterer's "not falling to bad destinies" (rising in destiny within the realm) and the once-returner's "one return" (fewer in number within the realm), only the non-returner's "no return to the desire-realm" is a crossing of the realm — the sole change of kind among the three rebirth-projections.

### 3.3 The water simile's crossing to the far shore: emerge, stand, observe, cross, arrive

The non-returner's crossing of the realm scripture depicts with the "water simile," an exceptionally apt image. The *Madhyama-āgama* Water-Simile sūtra lists seven kinds of person in water; the sixth kind figures the non-returner:

> 五下分結盡……生於彼間，便般涅槃，得不退法，不還此世，是謂有人出已而住，住已而觀，觀已而渡，渡已至彼岸……是謂第六水喻人。[^3]
>
> *"The five lower fetters exhausted… born in that place, he attains final nibbāna, gains the irreversible state, does not return to this world. This is the person who, having emerged, stands; having stood, observes; having observed, crosses; having crossed, reaches the far shore… This is the sixth water-simile person."*

The seven water-similes figure the depth of practice-and-realization by a person drowning, emerging, crossing a river. The non-returner is the "sixth water-simile person": emerges (out of the water), stands (steady), observes (the shore), crosses (over), *reaches the far shore*. The three words "reaches the far shore" depict exactly the crossing of the realm — the earlier water-simile persons either float or sink, or emerge and submerge again, none having left the water (figuring not yet out of the desire-realm's current); the non-returner has *crossed and gained the shore*, left the current itself. The simile's beauty is that it figures the change of kind "no return to the desire-realm" as the spatial crossing of "leaving the water and gaining the shore" — not swimming farther *in* the water (quantity) but getting *out* of the water (quality).

Closing through the approach-fruit grammar (continuing P02): the non-return *approach* is the moving aspect of advancing to sever the seventh through ninth grade of desire-realm cultivation-defilement (the lower three grades being severed, still mid-crossing); the non-return *fruit* is the resting aspect of having severed the ninth grade, the five lower collectively exhausted (already at the far shore, no return to the desire-realm). The fruit standing, "not returning to this world, ascending to the Pure Abodes" is what that fruit's resting aspect spreads out upon rebirth — and this spreading is uniquely the mark of crossing the realm. (The detailed five courses of final nibbāna in the Pure Abodes — in-between, on-rebirth, with-exertion, without-exertion, upstream — belong to the next paper, P07, "The Pure Abodes and the Five Courses of Final Nibbāna"; this paper halts at the single mark of "the threshold of the desire-realm.")

## V. Middle-Way Corrections

**❌ Misreading 1: "the non-returner is just the once-returner with three more grades severed."** The whole paper dismantles this. By count the non-returner is indeed the once-returner's continuation (severing the lower three grades), but the ninth grade severed precisely touches the critical point of desire-realm binding falling to zero, becoming a change of kind (crossing the realm, no return to the desire-realm). To read the non-returner as "a quantitative continuation" misses subtraction's non-linearity — the last grade severed not only subtracts one rebirth but crosses out of a realm.

**⚠️ Correction 2: "non-return" and "not falling" must not be conflated.** The stream-enterer's "not falling to lower destinies" is a rise in destiny *within* the realm (not falling to the desire-realm's bad destinies, still reborn in its good ones); the non-returner's "no return to the desire-realm" is a *crossing of the realm* (not reborn even in the desire-realm's good destinies, ascending to the form-realm). The one-character difference is the difference between within-the-realm and crossing-the-realm. Do not take the stream-enterer's "not falling" as equivalent to the non-returner's "non-return" — the former is still on this shore (the desire-realm), the latter has gained the far shore (the form-realm).

**⚠️ Correction 3: "ascending to the Pure Abodes" is neither the end of the non-returner's practice nor what this paper treats.** The non-returner ascends to the form-realm's Pure Abodes for final nibbāna, yet the five higher fetters (greed for form, greed for the formless, conceit, restlessness, ignorance) are not yet severed — he crosses the wall of the desire-realm, not the wall of the form- and formless-realms. So "no return to the desire-realm" is the threshold of leaving the desire-realm, not the end of the whole journey. How, after ascending, he attains final nibbāna in the Pure Abodes (the differences among the five courses) belongs to the next paper; this paper halts at the single mark of "crossing the desire-realm," not reaching the arahant-approach (the severance of the five higher fetters, P08). Do not take the non-returner as the ultimate.

## VI. Conclusion

**Essential restatement.** The non-returner's "five lower fetters exhausted, not returning to this world" is not the once-returner's mere quantitative continuation but the first change of kind subtraction produces. By count it is the continuation of severing the seventh through ninth grade of desire-realm cultivation-defilement, yet severing the ninth grade precisely zeroes the collective binding to the desire-realm (3.1); zeroed, rebirth changes in kind — from "a fixed number within the realm" (not-falling, one-return) it leaps to "crossing the realm, non-return," the sole kind-level among the three rebirth-projections (3.2); scripture depicts it with the water simile, the sixth water-simile person "having crossed, reaches the far shore," figuring exactly leaving the desire-realm's current and gaining the shore (3.3). The *Kośa*'s "severs the ninth, becomes the non-returner, must not be reborn in the desire-realm" is its treatise anchor. Subtraction accumulated to this point breaks through, for the first time, the wall of a "realm."

**Guidance for practice.** The understanding a practitioner should form of the non-returner is not the quantitative complacency "I have severed somewhat more," but a solemn recognition of "crossing the realm": the question is not "have my greed and hatred grown thinner," but "have the desire-realm-binding fetters been collectively exhausted, with not one part left able to summon me back to rebirth in the desire-realm." The former is still a within-realm reduction (the once-returner persists); the latter alone is the change of kind of crossing the realm (the non-returner achieved). Above all, remember: crossing the wall of the desire-realm is not crossing every wall — the five higher fetters remain, the binding of the form- and formless-realms still stands. The non-returner's "reaching the far shore" is having crossed the desire-realm's current, not having entered the remainderless; one who has gained the shore must still walk on.

**Open questions.** This paper cut Section IV because, though "quantity accumulated to a critical point becomes quality" formally resembles a phase transition, a phase transition is conditioned and reversible, alike in face but unlike in spirit with the realm-crossing of the cessation of the five lower fetters. A genuine question for later: how are "realm" (desire, form, formless) as a unit of binding and "grade" (the nine grades of cultivation-defilement) as a unit of severance to be unified within subtraction? The mechanism by which grade-by-grade severance, accumulated, becomes a crossing of the realm — its "critical point" — does it require importing the *Kośa*'s theory of realm-binding to be fully grounded? This is beyond this paper's threshold-theme. Further, this paper halts at the single mark of "leaving the desire-realm"; the next paper, P07, will treat where the non-returner goes *after* crossing — ascending to the Pure Abodes, whose final nibbāna has five courses (in-between, on-rebirth, with-exertion, without-exertion, upstream). "Non-return" is not uniform; there are five paths to arriving at nibbāna. The crossing is one change of kind; the five paths after the crossing are a new differentiation following the change of kind — this is what the next paper must unfold.
