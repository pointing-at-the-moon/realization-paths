# Appendix C · Glossary of Terms

In the body of the book, every technical term was explained in plain words at its first appearance; the Sanskrit and Pāli romanizations, with their dots and macrons, were all kept back here, so as not to disturb the text.

Each entry gives: **the term — romanization (P/S) — one plain sentence.** "P" marks Pāli (the idiom of the early āgama context), "S" marks Sanskrit (the idiom of the treatises); some terms carry both, and some are Chinese translators' own coinages, given without romanization.

---

## I. The Fruits, and Approach and Fruit

- **śramaṇa / renunciant** — samaṇa (P) / śramaṇa (S) — one gone forth to practice; the "four fruits of the renunciant's path" are this road's attainments.
- **sotāpanna / stream-enterer** — sotāpanna (P) / srota-āpanna (S) — the first fruit: the first three fetters severed, entered into the stream of the eightfold path, no falling to the lower destinies, at most seven returns.
- **sakadāgāmin / once-returner** — sakadāgāmin (P) / sakṛdāgāmin (S) — the second fruit: three fetters severed, greed-hatred-delusion attenuated (six grades of nine), at most one more return among humans.
- **anāgāmin / non-returner** — anāgāmin (P/S) — the third fruit: the five lower fetters exhausted, no return to the desire realm, born in the Pure Abodes unto final nibbāna.
- **arahant** — arahant (P) / arhat (S) — the fourth fruit: all ten fetters severed, the taints exhausted. This book writes only to its *approach*.
- **approach / fruit** — (Chinese: 向 / 果) — the two phases of one severance: the approach is the moving phase (coming down), the fruit the still phase (down for good).
- **the four pairs, eight persons** — (Chinese: 四雙八輩) — four approaches and four fruits, eight kinds of noble one; the sutta's content of "the World-Honored One's community of disciples."

## II. The Fetters — the Ruler's Markings

- **fetter** — saṃyojana (P/S) — a binding; the knot tying the mind to the round of birth and death. The fruits are measured by which are severed.
- **identity-view** — sakkāya-diṭṭhi (P) / satkāya-dṛṣṭi (S) — the view taking the five aggregates as "I." First of the three; blocks *who walks*.
- **doubt** — vicikicchā (P) / vicikitsā (S) — root hesitation toward the Buddha, Dharma, Saṅgha, and path. Second; blocks *whether one walks*.
- **grasping of rites and observances** — sīlabbata-parāmāsa (P) — clutching precepts and rituals irrelevant to liberation as the purifying way. Third; blocks *which road*.
- **sensual craving** — kāma-rāga (P/S) — craving for the desire realm's pleasures. Fourth of the lower five.
- **ill will** — paṭigha (P) / pratigha (S) — hostility toward what opposes. Fifth of the lower five.
- **the five lower fetters** — orambhāgiya-saṃyojana (P) — the five binding to the desire realm (the lower portion): identity-view, rite-grasping, doubt, sensual craving, ill will; their exhaustion is non-return.
- **craving for form** — rūpa-rāga (P) — the fine craving for the form realm's meditative bliss. First of the higher five.
- **craving for the formless** — arūpa-rāga (P) — the fine craving for the formless states. Second of the higher five.
- **conceit** — māna (P/S) — the last shadow of "I," most often the fine thought "I have attained." Third of the higher five.
- **restlessness** — uddhacca (P) / auddhatya (S) — the last unstilled stirring of a mind at utmost quiet. Fourth of the higher five.
- **ignorance** — avijjā (P) / avidyā (S) — the root of all fetters, lying under all knowing; severed last. Fifth of the higher five.
- **the five higher fetters** — uddhambhāgiya-saṃyojana (P) — the five binding to the upper two realms; the collected term belongs to the treatises, the content stands in the āgamas.
- **the nine grades** — (a treatise division) — the desire realm's greed-hatred-delusion divided from top-of-top to bottom-of-bottom; "attenuated" means six of nine permanently severed, the lower grade remaining.

## III. Severance and the Fruit's Substance

- **cessation through discernment** — pratisaṃkhyā-nirodha (S) — by the power of discerning wisdom, a defilement made never to arise again; that very "never again." Unconditioned, hence without regress. The substance of a fruit.
- **perception of impermanence** — anicca-saññā (P) — the perception that all (the bliss of absorption and "I" not excepted) is impermanent; the finest wisdom, severing the higher fetters at the root.
- **wisdom-liberated** — paññāvimutta (P) — the arahant without deep absorption whose taints are exhausted by wisdom; the class the World-Honored One ratified in the Susīma discourse.
- **both-ways liberated** — ubhatobhāgavimutta (P) — the arahant liberated on both sides, joining deep attainment to the taints' exhaustion.
- **taint** — āsava (P) / āsrava (S) — the seeping defilements; three in all: of sensuality, of being, of ignorance.
- **exhaustion of the taints** — (Chinese: 漏盡) — the three taints forever ended, the ten fetters done; the subtraction's completed aspect, the instant the arahant fruit stands.

## IV. Rebirth and the Realms

- **the three realms** — (Chinese: 欲界·色界·無色界) — desire, form, formless: the realm bound by sense pleasure, the realm of subtle form and meditative bliss, the realm of pure formless states. The lower five fetters bind to the first; the higher five to the upper two.
- **at most seven returns** — (Chinese: 極七返) — the stream-enterer's ceiling: at most seven births each among gods and humans (twenty-eight existences in all); "at most" means maximum, not allotment.
- **one return** — (Chinese: 一往來) — the once-returner's ceiling: once up among the gods, once back among humans, and there final nibbāna.
- **intermediate state** — antarābhava (S) — the passage-body between dying here and being born there; its existence is disputed among the schools; this book follows the Kośa's account.
- **the Pure Abodes** — suddhāvāsa (P) / śuddhāvāsa (S) — the uppermost five heavens of the fourth absorption: No Affliction, No Fever, Beautiful Appearance, Clear Sight, Akaniṣṭha; dwelt in by noble ones alone, the non-returner's birthplace.
- **Akaniṭṭha / the Limit of Form** — Akaniṭṭha (P) / Akaniṣṭha (S) — the summit of the Pure Abodes and of the form realm; the upstream attainer's end (the wisdom-inclined).
- **final nibbāna** — parinibbāna (P) / parinirvāṇa (S) — the final extinguishing.
- **the five final nibbānas** — (Chinese: 五種般涅槃) — the non-returner's five gaits to nibbāna: in the interval, upon rebirth, with exertion, without exertion, upstream. One fruit, differing journeys.
- **the four unshakable confidences** — (Chinese: 四不壞淨) — the stream-enterer's four marks: confidence in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṅgha that no longer shakes, with the noble virtues fulfilled.
- **the noble eightfold path** — ariya aṭṭhaṅgika magga (P) — right view through right concentration; the very stream that "stream-entry" enters.

## V. Other Terms

- **the four truths** — (Chinese: 四諦) — suffering, its arising, its cessation, the way; the seeing-as-it-is on which the knowledge of the taints' exhaustion rests.
- **the three knowledges** — (Chinese: 三明) — of past lives, of the divine eye, of the taints' exhaustion; the book quotes the third.
- **the self-declaration** — (Chinese: 自證定型句) — "Birth is exhausted; the holy life has been lived; what was to be done is done; there is no further becoming": the arahant's self-attestation at the taints' exhaustion, wholly in the language of finishing.
- **the limit of suffering** — (Chinese: 苦邊際) — suffering's continuity sealed at its edge; from here, no further suffering follows.
