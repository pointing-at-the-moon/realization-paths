# Appendix C · Glossary of Terms

In the main text, every term was glossed in plain words at its first appearance; the Sanskrit romanizations and diacritics are gathered here, so as not to disturb the text.

Each entry gives: **English rendering — Chinese — Sanskrit romanization — one plain sentence.** As a Mahāyāna volume, the romanizations are Sanskrit. Terms native to the Chinese translations, or names laid down by the later classifiers, carry no romanization and are marked (Ch.) or (classifiers' term).

---

## I. Positions and Names

- **Equal-awakening** — 等覺 — (Ch.) — equal to, on a level with, awakening, and not yet it; the position just before sublime-awakening, the subject of Part One.
- **Sublime-awakening** — 妙覺 — (Ch.) — the positional name of the fruit of buddhahood; the *Keyūra*'s forty-second ground, "the unsurpassed ground"; the ground where walking ends.
- **The stainless ground** — 無垢地 — (Ch.) — the *Keyūra*'s other name for the equal-awakening position; same characters as, but a different position from, the second of the ten grounds, the ground of freedom from stain (*vimalā*).
- **The ultimate stage** — 究竟位 — (Ch.) — the fifth and last of the Yogācāra five stages; abiding in unsurpassed perfect bodhi, the obscuration ended and the clarity full; naming the same fruit as sublime-awakening.
- **The Tathāgata's full awakening** — 如來成正覺 — (Ch.) — the *Avataṃsaka*'s name for the Buddha's ultimate awakening; without two, without mark, without walking, without halting; the same referent again.
- **The forty-two worthies and nobles** — 四十二賢聖 — (Ch.) — the *Keyūra*'s own positional frame: ten abodes, ten practices, ten dedications, ten grounds, the stainless ground, the ground of sublime-awakening.
- **The six lineage-natures / six firmnesses** — 六種性／六堅 — (Ch.) — the *Keyūra*'s natures gathering the positions: habituation, disposition, the way, the noble, equal-awakening, sublime-awakening.
- **The fifty-two positions** — 五十二位 — (classifiers' term) — the later systematizers' integer count (Guifeng; Tiantai counting the Ten Faiths in); borrowed in this book for convenience, with the scripture's own language standing wherever doctrine is decided.

## II. The Penultimate Birth and the Withholding

- **The penultimate birth** — 一生補處 — eka-jāti-pratibaddha — one span of life remaining, the next birth filling the Buddha's place; the most solid content of the equal-awakening position.
- **The bodhisattva bound to one birth** — 一生所繫菩薩 — (Ch.) — the *Avataṃsaka*'s phrase, synonymous with the penultimate birth.
- **Deliberately withholding realization** — 故留不證／留惑不證 — (Ch.) — realization within reach, held untaken for the sake of buddhahood; the defining mark of the penultimate birth.
- **Pratyekabuddha** — 辟支佛 — pratyekabuddha — the solitary awakened one; the fruit into which the penultimate birth would fall were the realization taken now.
- **The eighteen distinctive attributes** — 十八不共法 — (Ch.) — the eighteen qualities the Buddha alone possesses, shared with neither vehicle; the upper bound of "up through the eighteen distinctive attributes."
- **Unsurpassed perfect awakening** — 阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 — anuttarā samyak-saṃbodhi — what the ultimate stage abides in.

## III. The Concentration and the Severing

- **The adamantine concentration** — 金剛喻定 — vajropama-samādhi — the concentration likened to adamant; the last samādhi at the trainee's edge, crushing every defilement and crushed by none, severing the last nescience in one instant.
- **The adamantine mind / adamantine cognition** — 金剛心／金剛智 — (Ch.) — the keen cognition of equal-awakening; "the mark of nescience ending is the adamantine."
- **Sarvajñā** — 薩婆若 — sarvajñā (transliterated) — omniscience; "the ending-mark markless is sarvajñā," a name of the Buddha's knowing.
- **The beginningless-nescience abiding-ground** — 無始無明住地 — (Ch.) — the root nescience before which nothing further arises; the last thing severed; known by equal-awakening at its end, not at its beginning.
- **Primal nescience / the last grade of nescience** — 元品無明／最後一品無明 — (classifiers' term) — Tiantai and later usage for the last nescience severed; absent from the sources this book rests on, whose own word is "the beginningless-nescience abiding-ground."
- **The uninterrupted path / the path of liberation** — 無間道／解脫道 — (Ch.) — the thought in the act of severing / the thought that, severance done, realizes the cessation; the bodhisattva, of keen faculty, severs and realizes instant by instant, raising no two paths apart.
- **The trainee's last thought** — 學人末後心 — (Ch.) — the final thought of the trainee's stage; the adamantine concentration's place, the road's last thought as a road.
- **Instant** — 剎那 — kṣaṇa (transliterated) — the shortest measure of time; "in one instant the three realms severed at once."
- **Trainee / beyond training** — 有學／無學 — (Ch.) — one with something still to learn and sever / one with learning exhausted; the adamantine concentration arises at the trainee's outermost edge.
- **The two hindrances** — 二障 — kleśa-āvaraṇa / jñeya-āvaraṇa — the afflictive hindrance and the cognitive hindrance; their last innate subtleties severed at once when the adamantine concentration comes to presence.
- **Transformation of basis** — 轉依 — (Ch.) — the turning-over of the basis; "the buddha-fruit's perfected transformation-of-basis suddenly realized" when the concentration is present.

## IV. The Rest

- **The Dharma-Cloud Ground** — 法雲地 — dharmameghā — the tenth of the ten grounds; where the previous volume set down its pen, the station just before this volume begins.
- **The two vehicles** — 二乘 — (Ch.) — the śrāvaka and the pratyekabuddha; their adamantine-concentration context (the last thought before arhatship) differs from the Mahāyāna's (entering the Tathāgata-stage), recorded in this book without adjudication.
- **The obscuration ended, the clarity full** — 出障圓明 — (Ch.) — the fullness shown when the hindrances are out; the mark of the ultimate stage.
- **The omniscience of all modes** — 一切種智 — sarvākārajñatā — the Buddha's knowing at its ultimate; "the omniscience of all modes illumining beginning and end."
