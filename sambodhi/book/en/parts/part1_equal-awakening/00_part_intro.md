# Part One · Equal-Awakening · Standing by One Remainder

The ten grounds are full, the Dharma-Cloud spread; one step beyond is the Buddha. Yet between the ten grounds and the Buddha, the texts set one more position: equal-awakening.

The question comes at once: if the next step is buddhahood, what does this position in between have "more," to deserve a station of its own? Part One's two chapters answer against intuition: it has nothing more—it is off by exactly one. When the texts define equal-awakening, their eye is never on what it has amassed beyond the ten grounds, but on what still parts it from the Buddha; and that gap is exactly one portion: one nescience unexhausted, one span of birth unspent, one realization within reach and deliberately left untaken.

Chapter One, Off by One: how the name "equal" already means equal-and-not-yet-sublime; how "the penultimate birth" is marked by realization deliberately withheld; how the one remaining portion of nescience is known at its end and unknown at its beginning. Chapter Two, The Last Thought: the adamantine concentration that severs it—a concentration crushing all and crushed by none, root and branches ended in one instant; and the thought that severs, the road's last thought as a road.

The one that remains—look at it clearly first.
