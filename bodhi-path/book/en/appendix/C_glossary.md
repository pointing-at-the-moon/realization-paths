# Appendix C · Glossary of Terms

In the main text, every term was glossed in plain words at its first appearance; the Sanskrit romanizations and diacritics are gathered here, so as not to disturb the text.

Each entry gives: **English rendering — Chinese — Sanskrit romanization — one plain sentence.** As a Mahāyāna volume, the romanizations are Sanskrit. Terms native to the Chinese translations, or names laid down by the later classifiers, carry no romanization and are marked (Ch.) or (classifiers' term).

---

## I. The Gate and the Vehicle

- **Bodhicitta / arousing the mind** — 菩提心／發菩提心 — bodhicitta — the resolve to seek unsurpassed awakening for the liberation of all beings; the bodhisattva path's sole entrance, entered in one thought.
- **Bodhisattva** — 菩薩 — bodhisattva — one who has aroused bodhicitta and walks its path; counted among the bodhisattvas from the arising itself.
- **Great Vehicle** — 大乘 — mahāyāna — the vehicle whose aim is buddhahood for the sake of beings; a parallel track to the two vehicles, not their sequel.
- **Śrāvaka** — 聲聞 — śrāvaka — the hearer who cultivates the four fruits and takes realization; his road is the previous volume, *The Four Fruits of the Path*.
- **Pratyekabuddha** — 辟支佛／獨覺 — pratyekabuddha — the solitary awakened one; with the śrāvaka, the "two vehicles."
- **Turning from the lesser to the greater** — 回小向大 — (Ch.) — the notion of converting from the two vehicles to the Great Vehicle; the single-ladder misreading the first chapter dismantles attaches itself to this phrase.
- **Lineage** — 種姓 — gotra — the native capacity for buddhahood, innate and cultivated; the bodhisattva's own lineage, not a promotion earned by fruits.
- **All-knowledge** — 一切智 — sarvajñatā — the Buddha's unobstructed knowing; what the arising of the mind seeks.
- **Not taking realization / non-abiding** — 不取證／不住涅槃 — (Ch.) — arriving at the very place of realization and not taking it; the character of the whole path, against the śrāvaka's exit.
- **The two provisions** — 二資糧 — puṇya-jñāna-saṃbhāra — merit and wisdom, the two stores; what the road of accumulation amasses.
- **Three incalculable eons** — 三大阿僧祇劫 — asaṃkhyeya-kalpa — three spans of innumerable eons; the road's full length.
- **Perfection** — 波羅蜜 — pāramitā — "gone to the far shore"; the six earn the name only with prajñā leading.

## II. The Fifty Stations and the Non-Regresses

- **Ten Faiths** — 十信 — (part label: śraddhā, faith) — the first ten stations, outer-ordinary, before the abodes; the tidal zone where faith regresses.
- **Ten Abodes** — 十住 — (part label: sthāna, abode) — the second ten; entering the Buddha's house as a son of the Buddha; position-non-regress begins.
- **Ten Practices** — 十行 — (part label: caryā, practice) — the third ten; going out to benefit others, the six perfections enacted.
- **Ten Dedications** — 十迴向 — (part label: pariṇāmanā, dedication) — the fourth ten; turning the amassed good over; the close of the worthy ranks.
- **Ten Grounds** — 十地 — daśabhūmi — the fifth ten; the ten noble ones, where severance runs its course.
- **Outer-ordinary / three worthy / ten noble** — 外凡／三賢／十聖 — (Ch.) — the three tiers: the Ten Faiths outer-ordinary; abodes, practices, dedications the three worthy; the grounds the ten noble.
- **The three non-regresses** — 位不退／行不退／念不退 — (classifiers' term) — of position (from the abodes: no falling to the two vehicles or the ordinary), of practice (the dedications: the practice of benefit not retreating), of mindfulness (the eighth ground: the discriminating mind not stirring). The proper names are the classifiers'; the scriptures say "advancing portion," "never regressing," "avaivartika."
- **Avaivartika** — 阿鞞跋致 — avaivartika — non-regressing; the sources' own word for the non-regress.
- **Regressing portion / advancing portion** — 退分／上進分善根 — (Ch.) — the Keyūra's pair: the faith-position's roots can fall away; from the Abode of Arousing the Mind, they ascend without falling.
- **Bodhisattva in name** — 信想／假名／名字菩薩 — (Ch.) — the three provisional titles of one before the abodes; honest names for a standing not yet solid.
- **Son of the Buddha** — 佛子 — (Ch.) — one who has entered the family of the buddhas of the three times; the real title from the abodes onward.
- **Consecration** — 灌頂 — abhiṣeka — the Indian rite of royal investiture, water poured upon the crown; once at the end of the abodes, once at the end of the grounds.
- **Dharma-Prince** — 法王子 — (Ch.) — son of the Dharma-King; the ninth abode's name, and the ninth-ground bodhisattva's style.
- **Fallen within the number of the buddhas** — 墮在佛數 — (Ch.) — entered in the buddhas' register; the tenth ground's investiture mark—and not yet final buddhahood.

## III. The Six Perfections and the Dedication

- **The six perfections** — 六波羅蜜／六度 — ṣaṭ-pāramitā — giving, discipline, patience, energy, meditation, wisdom; the substance of the bodhisattva's practice.
- **Giving** — 檀／布施 — dāna — enacted as the Practice of Joy.
- **Discipline** — 尸羅／持戒 — śīla — enacted as the Practice of Benefiting.
- **Patience** — 羼提／忍辱 — kṣānti — enacted as the Practice of Non-Opposition.
- **Energy** — 毘梨耶／精進 — vīrya — enacted as the Practice of Indomitability.
- **Meditation** — 禪／禪定 — dhyāna — enacted as the Practice of Freedom from Confusion (right mindfulness).
- **Wisdom** — 般若 — prajñā — the guide of the five; without it, the five are blind.
- **Merit-portion and wisdom-portion** — 福智二分 — (Ch.) — the six perfections' two threads: giving, discipline, patience to merit; prajñā to wisdom; energy and meditation through both.
- **Non-attainment** — 無所得 — (Ch.) — prajñā's working mark: no hold taken on giver, receiver, gift; the ground for matching Excellent Manifestation to wisdom.
- **Dedication** — 迴向 — pariṇāmanā — the turning-over of what is amassed; redirecting the good from settling into self-fruit.
- **The threefold dedication** — 三處迴向 — (classifiers' synthesis) — to beings, to bodhi, to the real; a later synthesis of the chapter's dispersed showings.
- **Suchness** — 真如 — tathatā — the way things are; what the eighth dedication accords with, and what the path of seeing beholds.
- **Good spiritual friend** — 善知識 — kalyāṇa-mitra — the friend on whom the faith-position's destination hangs.

## IV. The Grounds and the Severances

- **Ground of Joy** — 歡喜地 — pramuditā — the first ground; first realizing the noble's place; Xuanzang: "Supreme Joy."
- **Ground of Stainlessness** — 離垢地 — vimalā — the second; the stain of transgression left behind.
- **Light-Emitting Ground** — 發光地 — prabhākarī — the third; the dharmas shining forth; the treatise glosses "Radiance."
- **Ground of Blazing Wisdom** — 焰慧地 — arciṣmatī — the fourth; wisdom-fire burning the fuel of afflictions; the treatise: "Flaming."
- **Hard-to-Conquer Ground** — 難勝地 — sudurjayā — the fifth; world-transcending wisdom ferrying the hard-to-ferry.
- **Manifest Ground** — 現前地 — abhimukhī — the sixth; its gloss not unfolded in this book.
- **Far-Reaching Ground** — 遠行地 — dūraṃgamā — the seventh; passing beyond the place where the two vehicles stop.
- **Immovable Ground** — 不動地 — acalā — the eighth; unbreakable, unmovable; application cast off for spontaneity.
- **Ground of Good Wisdom** — 善慧地 — sādhumatī — the ninth; unobstructed teaching, the benefit of others accomplished.
- **Dharma-Cloud Ground** — 法雲地 — dharmameghā — the tenth; the great dharma-body like a covering cloud; the second consecration.
- **Path of seeing / path of cultivation** — 見道／修道 — darśana-mārga / bhāvanā-mārga — the first ground's direct beholding of suchness / the later grounds' gradual severing; the two times of the ground-architecture.
- **Bonds of view** — 見縛 — (Ch.) — the afflictions the path of seeing severs: born of discrimination and wrong view, dead the moment the principle is seen.
- **Discriminated / innate afflictions** — 分別惑／俱生惑 — (classifiers' term) — the later Yogācāra names for what seeing and cultivation sever; the sources say "bonds of view," "afflictions of the path of cultivation."
- **Suppression / severance** — 伏／斷 — (Ch.) — the stone on the grass against the root pulled out; the worthy suppress, the noble sever.
- **The ten obstacles** — 十障 — (Ch.) — one countered by each ground, coarse to fine: the mark of self, wrong conduct, forgetting, conceit of understanding, conceit of purity, subtle afflictive habit, fine-mark habit, applied effort amid the markless, failing to benefit beings, lacking mastery over dharmas.
- **Forbearance of the non-arising of dharmas** — 無生法忍 — anutpattika-dharma-kṣānti — the settled consent that dharmas neither arise nor cease, unbreakable and unmovable; its accomplishment is the eighth ground's gate. "Forbearance" here is acceptance, not endurance.
- **Non-application** — 無功用 — anābhoga — without deliberate exertion; the eighth ground's casting off of applied practice.
- **Spontaneity** — 任運 — (Ch.) — running of itself, unforced; Xuanzang's word for the eighth ground.
- **The ten masteries** — 十自在 — (Ch.) — the eighth-ground bodhisattva's: over life, mind, wealth, action, birth, vow, understanding, will, wisdom, dharma (Xuanzang's renderings differ slightly); countering ten terrors. The bodhisattva's roster, distinct from the buddhas' own ten masteries.
- **The two obstructions** — 煩惱障／所知障 — kleśa-āvaraṇa / jñeya-āvaraṇa — the affliction-obstruction barring liberation, and the knowledge-obstruction veiling all-knowledge; the two vehicles purify the first, the bodhisattva lineage both.
- **The subtle obstacle of cognition** — 微智障 — (Ch.) — the last fine veil remaining above the tenth ground; for its counteraction the buddha-ground is spoken of separately.
- **Equal awakening / sublime awakening** — 等覺／妙覺 — (classifiers' term) — the two stations the later classifiers set between the tenth ground and the buddha-ground; the matter of this series' next volume.

## V. Others

- **The thirty-seven factors of the way** — 三十七道品 — (Ch.) — the śrāvaka's curriculum, which the bodhisattva fully contemplates without taking realization.
- **Keeping a residue to moisten rebirth** — 留惑潤生 — (Ch.) — the bodhisattva's device of not severing to the final exit, retaining a trace for the sake of rebirth among beings.
- **Anuttarā samyak-saṃbodhi** — 阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 — anuttarā samyak-saṃbodhi — unsurpassed, perfect awakening; the fruit the arising of the mind seeks.
- **Dharma-body** — 法身 — dharmakāya — the body of the Dharma; what the Dharma-Cloud Ground attains "complete in mastery."
- **Omniscience in all modes** — 一切種智 — sarvākārajñatā — the Buddha's knowing in its finality; to which the dedication of suchness consigns all roots of good.
