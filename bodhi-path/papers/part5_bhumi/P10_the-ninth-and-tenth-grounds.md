# The Ninth and Tenth Grounds

**The Ground of Good Wisdom · the perfection of power · the unobstructed power of teaching accomplishing the practice of benefiting others · the Dharma-Cloud Ground · the perfection of wisdom · attaining the great dharma-body with mastery complete · the consecration that confers office, falling within the number of the buddhas · yet a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining, the buddha-ground spoken of separately**

*中文主題：九地十地——善慧·法雲·灌頂受職與近佛之際*

## Abstract

The preceding paper, BODHI-P09, carried the bodhisattva to the eighth Immovable Ground — realizing the forbearance of non-arising, casting off application and becoming spontaneous, mindfulness-non-regress and accomplishing the bodhisattva's ten masteries; yet the buddhas exhorted that "the ten powers, the fearlessnesses, the eighteen distinctive dharmas of the buddhas that we possess, you have not yet attained," so the eighth ground is non-regressing yet the causal stage not complete. This paper takes up that thread, treating the last two of the ten grounds — the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom and the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground — and the close of the ten grounds, the brink of buddhahood. This paper argues: the last two grounds are the course in which the bodhisattva's causal-stage "function of benefiting others and substance of wisdom" ripens, and the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground's consecration that confers office is the final stage of "near buddhahood yet not yet buddhahood." The ninth Ground of Good Wisdom has the "perfection of power" predominant and is named for "the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others" — the eighth ground's self-realization being firm, it broadens into the mastery of benefiting others by unobstructed teaching; the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground has the "perfection of wisdom" predominant, the matching of the ten perfections here consummated, and is named for "attaining the great dharma-body, mastery complete" — the dharma-body like a cloud, covering and moistening all. The tenth-ground bodhisattva enters the "position of conferred office (consecration)," the buddhas' water of wisdom anointing his crown, "by fulfilling the ten powers of the tathāgata, he falls within the number of the buddhas," likened to a wheel-turning sage-king anointing the crown of the crown prince with the water of the four great oceans to confer the kingly office. Yet the boundary must be held: though he falls within the number of the buddhas and fulfills the ten powers, Vasubandhu still judges that "here there is a subtle obstacle of cognition, so there is no mastery; to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" — above the tenth ground there is yet a last subtle obstacle of cognition unexhausted, so the buddha-ground is spoken of separately. The evidence is in three texts: the *Eighty-fascicle Avataṃsaka*'s "Chapter on the Ten Grounds," the *Daśabhūmika-sūtra* (Xuanzang's translation), and Vasubandhu's *Daśabhūmika-vyākhyāna*. From these the paper argues three things: the last two grounds as the ripening of benefiting-others and wisdom (3.1); the consecration that confers office and falling within the number of the buddhas as the brink of buddhahood (3.2); and the yet-remaining subtle obstacle of cognition and the buddha-ground spoken of separately as the close of the causal stage (3.3). An honest mark: the relation of the Ground of Good Wisdom's "unobstructed teaching" to the later-matched "four unobstructed discernments (of dharma, meaning, expression, eloquence)" is not the express wording of this round's three sources, and is not supplied; "the awakening-of-equivalence and the wondrous awakening" as proper names of stages between the ten grounds and the buddha-ground are a later (Yingluo-jing / Tiantai) teaching-classification, not the express word of the three sources, and this paper does not presume to judge them; the source unit's D-3 (the T0279 closing sentence of the Dharma-Cloud Ground's conferred office) is not included or fabricated, its line unverified. The conclusion: the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground is the close of the bodhisattva's causal stage, the brink of buddhahood where, by the consecration that confers office, he falls within the number of the buddhas, yet with a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining and the buddha-ground spoken of separately — this too is the close of the Realization-axis *Bodhi Path* (from the ten faiths to the ten grounds), its buddha-ground above (the tathāgata's mastery with the subtle obstacle severed) seaming to the Attainment-axis's final division, for the author to adjudicate.

## I. The Question

The preceding paper carried the bodhisattva to the eighth Immovable Ground. Realizing the forbearance of non-arising, casting off application and becoming spontaneous, mindfulness-non-regress and accomplishing the bodhisattva's ten masteries — already a most exalted noble stage. Yet at its close that paper left a decisive scriptural admonition: the buddhas, exhorting the eighth-ground bodhisattva when he attained the forbearance, said, "the ten powers, the fearlessnesses, the eighteen distinctive dharmas of the buddhas that we possess, you have not yet attained." Though the eighth ground is non-regressing, spontaneous, and masterful, the buddhas' ten powers and fearlessnesses are still "not yet attained" — so the eighth ground is non-regressing yet the causal stage not complete (BODHI-P09).

So the close of the ten grounds presses out two questions. First, after the eighth ground come still the ninth and tenth grounds — what do these last two grounds do? Answer: they are the ripening of the bodhisattva's causal-stage "function of benefiting others" and "substance of wisdom." The ninth Ground of Good Wisdom has the "perfection of power" predominant and is named for "the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others" — the eighth ground's self-realization being firm, it broadens into the mastery of benefiting others by unobstructed teaching; the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground has the "perfection of wisdom" predominant, the matching of the ten perfections here consummated, and is named for "attaining the great dharma-body, mastery complete" — the dharma-body like a cloud, covering and moistening all. From the eighth ground's "self-realization, effortless and spontaneous," it advances to the ninth ground's "benefiting others by unobstructed teaching" and the tenth ground's "great dharma-body with mastery complete": the self-realization being firm, it broadens into the function of benefiting others and rounds out the substance of wisdom.

Second, and the sharper question: the tenth-ground Dharma-Cloud bodhisattva enters the "position of conferred office by consecration," the buddhas' water of wisdom anointing his crown, "by fulfilling the ten powers of the tathāgata, he falls within the number of the buddhas" — since he "falls within the number of the buddhas" and "fulfills the ten powers," is this not already the attainment of buddhahood? This question presses out the last boundary this paper must hold. Answer: near buddhahood, yet not yet buddhahood. The tenth ground's consecration that confers office is likened to a wheel-turning sage-king anointing the crown of his crown prince with the water of the four great oceans to confer the kingly office — the anointed prince already falls within the number of the anointed kṣatriya kings, already bears the marks of a king, yet is not yet the reigning king. Vasubandhu judges still more plainly: even at this ground, "here there is a subtle obstacle of cognition, so there is no mastery; to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" — above the tenth ground there is yet a last "subtle obstacle of cognition" unexhausted, so the buddha-ground must be spoken of separately. So the tenth ground is the close of the causal stage, the brink of buddhahood; the severing of the subtle obstacle of cognition above it and the realizing of the buddha-fruit are the affair of another division (the Attainment-axis's final division). The ripening of the last two grounds, the near-buddhahood of the consecration that confers office, the yet-remaining subtle obstacle of cognition — these three and one boundary are what this paper sets out to unfold; and here, too, the whole course of the Realization-axis *Bodhi Path*, from the ten faiths, ten abodes, ten practices, and ten dedications up to the ten grounds, comes to its close.

## II. The Canonical Basis

This section sets out four sets of textual evidence: the full listing of the ten grounds (the last two at its close); the names and perfections of the last two grounds; the Dharma-Cloud Ground's consecration that confers office, falling within the number of the buddhas; and the receiving of the dharma-king's office with a subtle obstacle of cognition yet remaining. All citations are collated against the CBETA electronic canon, in three texts — the *Eighty-fascicle Avataṃsaka* (T0279), the *Daśabhūmika-sūtra* (T0287), and Vasubandhu's *Daśabhūmika-vyākhyāna* (T1522).

### 2.1 The full listing of the ten grounds: Good Wisdom and the Dharma-Cloud at its close

The *Eighty-fascicle Avataṃsaka*'s "Chapter on the Ten Grounds" formally lists the full names of the bodhisattva's ten grounds, Good Wisdom and the Dharma-Cloud the last two:

> 何等為十？一者歡喜地，二者離垢地，三者發光地，四者焰慧地，五者難勝地，六者現前地，七者遠行地，八者不動地，九者善慧地，十者法雲地。佛子！此菩薩十地，三世諸佛已說、當說、今說。

*What are the ten? First, the Ground of Joy; second, the Ground of Stainlessness; third, the Light-Emitting Ground; fourth, the Blazing-Wisdom Ground; fifth, the Hard-to-Conquer Ground; sixth, the Manifest Ground; seventh, the Far-Reaching Ground; eighth, the Immovable Ground; ninth, the Ground of Good Wisdom; tenth, the Dharma-Cloud Ground. O child of the Buddha! These ten grounds of the bodhisattva the buddhas of the three times have spoken, will speak, and now speak.* (*Mahāvaipulya-buddhāvataṃsaka-sūtra*, fasc. 34, "Chapter on the Ten Grounds, 26, part 1," T10n0279_p0179b22, trans. Śikṣānanda)

The *Daśabhūmika-sūtra* (Xuanzang's translation) lists the ten grounds alike, the last two agreeing with the Avataṃsaka tradition:

> 何等為十？一名極喜地……九名善慧，十名法雲。唯諸佛子！此名一切菩薩十地。

*What are the ten? First, named the Ground of Supreme Joy … ninth, Good Wisdom; tenth, Dharma-Cloud. O children of the Buddha! These are named the ten grounds of all bodhisattvas.* (*Daśabhūmika-sūtra*, fasc. 1, "The Bodhisattva's Ground of Supreme Joy, 1, part 1," T10n0287_p0536b03, trans. Xuanzang)

The two renderings side by side show: the names of the last two grounds (Good Wisdom · Dharma-Cloud) agree in both translations. The ninth, Good Wisdom, and the tenth, Dharma-Cloud, are the last two of the ten grounds — the close of the causal stage, after the eighth Immovable Ground and before the buddha-ground.

### 2.2 The names and perfections of the last two grounds: Good Wisdom's perfection of power, the Dharma-Cloud's perfection of wisdom

Vasubandhu's *Daśabhūmika-vyākhyāna* glosses each ground's name; the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom is named for "the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others," the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground for "attaining the great dharma-body, mastery complete":

> 無礙力說法，成就利他行，故名善慧地。得大法身具足自在，故名法雲地。

*By the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others — hence named the Ground of Good Wisdom. Attaining the great dharma-body, mastery complete — hence named the Dharma-Cloud Ground.* (*Daśabhūmika-vyākhyāna*, fasc. 2, "Gloss on the Meaning of the Names of the Ten Grounds," T26n1522_p0127a17; same fasc., T26n1522_p0127a27)

Each of the last two grounds has its predominant perfection. The ninth Ground of Good Wisdom has the "perfection of power" predominant:

> 是菩薩十波羅蜜中力波羅蜜增上，餘波羅蜜非不修習，隨力隨分。佛子！是名略說菩薩第九菩薩善慧地。

*Among the ten perfections of this bodhisattva, the perfection of power is predominant; the remaining perfections are not uncultivated, but according to capacity and measure. O child of the Buddha! This is the brief account of the bodhisattva's ninth bodhisattva Ground of Good Wisdom.* (*Daśabhūmika-vyākhyāna*, citing the sūtra, fasc. 11, "The Ninth Ground of Good Wisdom," T26n1522_p0193a24)

The tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground has the "perfection of wisdom" predominant, the matching of the ten perfections here consummated:

> 是菩薩十波羅蜜中智波羅蜜增上。佛子！是名略說菩薩第十菩薩法雲地。

*Among the ten perfections of this bodhisattva, the perfection of wisdom is predominant. O child of the Buddha! This is the brief account of the bodhisattva's tenth bodhisattva Dharma-Cloud Ground.* (*Daśabhūmika-vyākhyāna*, citing the sūtra, fasc. 12, "The Tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground," T26n1522_p0200a23)

The three texts together: the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom's name-meaning is "the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others," its predominant perfection the "perfection of power"; the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground's name-meaning is "attaining the great dharma-body, mastery complete," its predominant perfection the "perfection of wisdom." The ten grounds match the ten perfections, and at the Dharma-Cloud Ground's perfection of wisdom the matching is consummated. Good Wisdom's "benefiting others by unobstructed teaching" is the ripening of the function of benefiting others; the Dharma-Cloud's "great dharma-body with mastery complete" is the ripening of the substance of wisdom — the name "Dharma-Cloud" taking just the sense of "the great dharma-body like a cloud, covering and moistening all."

### 2.3 The Dharma-Cloud Ground's consecration that confers office: falling within the number of the buddhas, the wheel-king anointing the crown prince

The *Eighty-fascicle Avataṃsaka* shows the Dharma-Cloud Ground bodhisattva entering the "position of conferred office (consecration)," the buddhas' water of wisdom anointing his crown, falling within the number of the buddhas, with the wheel-turning sage-king's anointing of the crown prince as its likeness:

> 此菩薩得先所未得百萬三昧，名為：已得受職之位，入佛境界，具足十力，墮在佛數。佛子！如轉輪聖王所生太子……取四大海水置金瓶內，王執此瓶灌太子頂，是時即名：受王職位，墮在灌頂剎利王數……菩薩受職亦復如是，諸佛智水灌其頂故，名為：受職；具足如來十種力故，墮在佛數。

*This bodhisattva attains a million samādhis not attained before, which is called: having attained the position of conferred office, entering the realm of the buddhas, fulfilling the ten powers, falling within the number of the buddhas. O child of the Buddha! Just as the crown prince born to a wheel-turning sage-king … takes the water of the four great oceans, places it in a golden vase, and the king, holding this vase, anoints the crown of the prince — at that time it is called: receiving the kingly office, falling within the number of the anointed kṣatriya kings … the bodhisattva's receiving of office is likewise: because the buddhas' water of wisdom anoints his crown, it is called receiving office; because he fulfills the ten powers of the tathāgata, he falls within the number of the buddhas.* (*Mahāvaipulya-buddhāvataṃsaka-sūtra*, fasc. 39, "Chapter on the Ten Grounds, 26, part 6 · the Tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground," T10n0279_p0206a18)

This passage is the pivotal evidence for the close of the ten grounds, the brink of buddhahood. The Dharma-Cloud Ground bodhisattva enters the "position of conferred office" — "receiving office" being like a wheel-turning sage-king anointing the crown of the crown prince born to him with the water of the four great oceans, at which time the prince "receives the kingly office, falls within the number of the anointed kṣatriya kings"; the bodhisattva's receiving of office is likewise, "because the buddhas' water of wisdom anoints his crown, it is called receiving office; because he fulfills the ten powers of the tathāgata, he falls within the number of the buddhas." "Falling within the number of the buddhas" means being listed in the number (the rank) of the buddhas; "fulfilling the ten powers" means fulfilling the ten powers of the tathāgata. Compared with the eighth ground's "the buddhas' ten powers and fearlessnesses you have not yet attained," the tenth ground's consecration that confers office is a great advance, already near to the buddhas.

### 2.4 Receiving the dharma-king's office, yet a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining: the buddha-ground spoken of separately

Yet Vasubandhu, at the close of his gloss of the names, plainly judges that above the tenth ground there is yet a last subtle obstacle of cognition unexhausted, so the buddha-ground is spoken of separately:

> 如是受法王位，猶如太子於諸王子而得自在，是處有微智障故不自在，對治此障故說佛地。

*Thus receiving the dharma-king's office, like the crown prince attaining mastery among the king's sons, yet here there is a subtle obstacle of cognition, so there is no mastery; to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately.* (*Daśabhūmika-vyākhyāna*, fasc. 2, "Gloss on the Meaning of the Names of the Ten Grounds · the Close," T26n1522_p0127a29)

Vasubandhu, with "receiving the dharma-king's office, like the crown prince attaining mastery among the king's sons," shows the position of the Dharma-Cloud Ground — already like the crown prince attaining mastery among the king's sons (already received office, near the kingly position); yet "here there is a subtle obstacle of cognition, so there is no mastery" — at this there is yet a last "subtle obstacle of cognition," so the ultimate mastery is not attained; "to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" — to counteract this subtle obstacle of cognition, the "buddha-ground" is then spoken of separately. This sentence decisively marks: between the close of the ten grounds and the buddha-ground there is still the interval of one subtle obstacle of cognition; the tenth ground's consecration that confers office, though it falls within the number of the buddhas, is still not the ultimate buddha-fruit that severs this subtle obstacle. (Note: the source unit holds besides a D-3, an Avataṃsaka closing sentence "the Dharma-Cloud Ground's office received … named abiding in the Dharma-Cloud Ground"; because its T0279 line was not verified, this paper neither cites nor fabricates its text, see Section V.)

## III. The Core Argument

This section unfolds in three layers the doctrine of the last two grounds and the close of the ten grounds: the last two grounds as the ripening of benefiting-others and wisdom (3.1); the consecration that confers office and falling within the number of the buddhas as the brink of buddhahood (3.2); the yet-remaining subtle obstacle of cognition and the buddha-ground spoken of separately as the close of the causal stage (3.3).

### 3.1 The last two grounds: the ripening of the function of benefiting others and the substance of wisdom

The last two grounds after the eighth are the final ripening of the bodhisattva's causal-stage "function" and "substance." The eighth ground's realization is the firmness of "self-realization" — the forbearance of non-arising, effortless spontaneity, mindfulness-non-regress, all inclining toward the bodhisattva's own accomplishment of realization. Yet the bodhisattva path does not stop at self-realization; its vow is in benefiting others, its consummation in the rounding of wisdom. The last two grounds are precisely this close in which, "the self-realization being firm, it broadens into the function of benefiting others and rounds out the substance of wisdom."

First, the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom. Its predominant perfection is the "perfection of power" (§2.2), its name-meaning "the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others" (§2.2). The eighth ground having attained effortless, spontaneous self-realization, the ninth ground can then, by the "unobstructed power," teach the dharma — teaching unobstructed, benefiting others with mastery, "accomplishing the practice of benefiting others." This is the ripening of the bodhisattva's "function of benefiting others": the power of self-realization being full, it issues as the function of unobstructed teaching, broadly benefiting beings. Next, the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground. Its predominant perfection is the "perfection of wisdom" (§2.2), the matching of the ten perfections here consummated; its name-meaning is "attaining the great dharma-body, mastery complete" (§2.2). The name "Dharma-Cloud" most repays pondering — the great dharma-body like a cloud, covering and moistening all. The perfection of wisdom being predominant, the dharma-body's mastery complete, like a great cloud covering all, pouring down the dharma-rain to moisten the host of beings. This is the ripening of the bodhisattva's "substance of wisdom": the wisdom of self-realization being rounded, it becomes the substance of the great dharma-body, mastery complete.

So the sequence of the last two grounds is the successive ripening of "function" and "substance": the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom accomplishes the function of benefiting others (unobstructed teaching), the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground rounds the substance of wisdom (the great dharma-body, mastery complete). From the eighth ground's firmness of self-realization, to the ninth ground's function of benefiting others and the tenth ground's substance of wisdom — this is the bodhisattva's last ripening in the causal stage.

A contrast: the accomplished teacher. A learner first studies to thorough mastery (like the eighth ground's effortless self-realization) — this is "self-realization"; the study being thorough, he can then teach others without obstruction, answering questions and resolving doubts (like the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom's unobstructed teaching, benefiting others) — this is the "function of benefiting others"; and coming to a rich learning, a school of his own, a transformation like the timely rain, its reach broad (like the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground's great dharma-body with mastery complete, like a cloud covering all) — this is the ripening of "substance." Self-study mastered (the eighth ground), able to teach (the ninth ground), the rain of transformation moistening all alike (the tenth ground) — just this sequence of "self-realization — benefiting others — rounded substance." The ripening of the last two grounds may be seen herein.

> ✅ Doctrinally sound: the last two grounds are the ripening of the function of benefiting others and the substance of wisdom. The ninth Ground of Good Wisdom has the "perfection of power" predominant, named "the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others" (§2.2) — the eighth ground's self-realization being firm, it issues as the mastery of benefiting others by unobstructed teaching (the ripening of function); the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground has the "perfection of wisdom" predominant, the matching of the ten perfections consummated, named "attaining the great dharma-body, mastery complete" (§2.2) — the substance of wisdom being rounded, it becomes the great dharma-body like a cloud covering all (the ripening of substance). From the eighth ground's firm self-realization, to the ninth ground's function of benefiting others and the tenth ground's substance of wisdom, is the bodhisattva's last ripening in the causal stage. Like the sequence of the learner who masters his study, can teach others, and at last moistens all like the timely rain.

### 3.2 The consecration that confers office, falling within the number of the buddhas: the brink of buddhahood

The second aspect of the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground is the "consecration that confers office" — this is the pivot of the close of the ten grounds, the brink of buddhahood. The Dharma-Cloud Ground bodhisattva enters the "position of conferred office," the buddhas anointing his crown with the water of wisdom, "because he fulfills the ten powers of the tathāgata, he falls within the number of the buddhas" (§2.3). The text takes the wheel-turning sage-king's anointing of the crown prince as its likeness: the wheel-king takes the water of the four great oceans, places it in a golden vase, and anoints the crown of the crown prince born to him, and the prince at that time "receives the kingly office, falls within the number of the anointed kṣatriya kings"; the bodhisattva's receiving of office is likewise — "because the buddhas' water of wisdom anoints his crown, it is called receiving office."

The two phrases "falling within the number of the buddhas" and "fulfilling the ten powers" mark the tenth ground's nearness to the buddhas. "Falling within the number of the buddhas" means being listed in the number, the rank, of the buddhas; "fulfilling the ten powers" means fulfilling the tathāgata's ten powers. Looking back to the eighth ground, the buddhas still admonished "the buddhas' ten powers and fearlessnesses you have not yet attained" (BODHI-P09); at the tenth ground's consecration that confers office, then, "because he fulfills the ten powers of the tathāgata, he falls within the number of the buddhas" — from the eighth ground's "not yet attained" to the tenth ground's "fulfilling, falling within the number of the buddhas," one sees just the course of the eighth, ninth, and tenth grounds drawing near to the buddhas. The tenth ground's consecration that confers office is the highest aspect the close of the causal stage can reach: already anointed by the buddhas' water of wisdom, already fulfilling the virtue of the tathāgata's ten powers, already listed in the number of the buddhas.

A contrast: the crown prince anointed. The crown prince of a wheel-turning sage-king receives the office by anointing — the king takes the water of the four oceans and anoints his crown, and the prince thereupon receives the kingly office, falls within the number of the anointed kṣatriya kings. The anointed prince already bears the marks of a king (the anointing water, the heir's position), already falls within the number of the kings, and is about to be king. The tenth-ground bodhisattva's consecration that confers office is just this position of "the heir receiving office": the buddhas' water of wisdom anointing him, like the water of the four oceans; falling within the number of the buddhas, like falling within the number of the kṣatriya kings. Already received office, already bearing the marks of a king (a buddha), already listed in the number of the buddhas — this is the position of "about to be a buddha."

> ✅ Doctrinally sound: the consecration that confers office, falling within the number of the buddhas, is the brink of buddhahood. The Dharma-Cloud Ground bodhisattva enters the "position of conferred office," the buddhas' water of wisdom anointing his crown, "because he fulfills the ten powers of the tathāgata, he falls within the number of the buddhas" (§2.3), likened to the wheel-king anointing the crown prince with the water of the four oceans, who "receives the kingly office, falls within the number of the anointed kṣatriya kings." The two phrases "falling within the number of the buddhas" (listed in the rank of the buddhas) and "fulfilling the ten powers" (fulfilling the tathāgata's ten powers) mark its nearness to the buddhas — a great advance over the eighth ground's "the buddhas' ten powers you have not yet attained," showing the course of the eighth, ninth, and tenth grounds drawing near to the buddhas. Like the prince anointed, falling within the number of the kṣatriya kings, about to be king, the tenth ground's consecration that confers office is the highest causal-stage aspect of "about to be a buddha."

### 3.3 The yet-remaining subtle obstacle of cognition, the buddha-ground spoken of separately: the close of the causal stage

Yet here this paper must hold the last boundary — and this is also the closing boundary of the whole *Bodhi Path* Realization-axis. Though the tenth ground's consecration confers office, falls within the number of the buddhas, and fulfills the ten powers, Vasubandhu still plainly judges: "thus receiving the dharma-king's office, like the crown prince attaining mastery among the king's sons, yet here there is a subtle obstacle of cognition, so there is no mastery; to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" (§2.4). This sentence must be read layer by layer. "Receiving the dharma-king's office, like the crown prince attaining mastery among the king's sons" — the tenth ground's position is like the crown prince attaining mastery among the king's sons (already received office, near the kingly position); "here there is a subtle obstacle of cognition, so there is no mastery" — yet at this there is still a last "subtle obstacle of cognition," so the ultimate mastery is not attained; "to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" — to counteract this subtle obstacle of cognition, the "buddha-ground" is then spoken of separately.

So "falling within the number of the buddhas" is not "being the ultimate buddha-fruit." "Falling within the number of the buddhas" is like the anointed prince's "falling within the number of the anointed kṣatriya kings" — already bearing the king's qualifications, already listed in the number of the kings, yet the anointed prince is not yet the reigning king; between them is still the interval of a last stage. The tenth-ground bodhisattva's consecration that confers office is just this position of "already falling within the number of the buddhas yet not yet the ultimate buddha": above it there is still a last subtle obstacle of cognition unsevered, and to sever this obstacle is to realize the buddha-ground. Vasubandhu's sentence "to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" decisively divides "the tenth ground" from "the buddha-ground" as two affairs — the tenth ground is the close of the causal stage, the buddha-ground spoken of separately above it.

This is the last fulfilment of this axis's sense of the bodhisattva path as a course. From BODHI-P01's establishing of the bodhisattva path as not continuing from the two vehicles, having its own causal-stage course, through the ten faiths, ten abodes, ten practices, ten dedications (the three worthy ranks suppressing afflictions), the first ground's path of seeing (severing the discriminated afflictions), the second to seventh grounds' path of cultivation (gradually severing the innate afflictions), the eighth ground's effortless spontaneity (mindfulness-non-regress yet the buddhas' ten powers not yet attained), the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom (benefiting others by unobstructed teaching), the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground (the consecration that confers office, falling within the number of the buddhas) — this whole road of the causal stage reaches its utmost at the tenth ground's consecration that confers office, yet with a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining and the buddha-ground spoken of separately. The bodhisattva path's causal-stage course here comes to its full close; the severing of the subtle obstacle of cognition above it and the realizing of the tathāgata's mastery as the buddha-fruit are the affair spoken of separately by the Attainment-axis's final division. The close of the ten grounds is precisely the seam between the Realization-axis *Bodhi Path* and the Attainment-axis's buddha-fruit.

A contrast: the last stretch before the summit. Having passed the whole course and drawn near the summit — already able to see the very peak (falling within the number of the buddhas), already bearing the qualifications and gear for the summit (fulfilling the ten powers, the consecration that confers office), yet beneath the feet there is still a last stretch unclimbed (the subtle obstacle of cognition unsevered). The anointed prince already bears the king's qualifications yet is still before the enthronement; the near-summit climber already has the prospect of the very peak yet is still before the last step. The tenth ground's consecration that confers office is just this last stretch of "already near yet not yet arrived, already having the qualifications yet not yet ultimate"; to cross this last subtle obstacle of cognition is the buddha-ground.

> ✅ Doctrinally sound: the yet-remaining subtle obstacle of cognition, the buddha-ground spoken of separately, is the close of the causal stage. Vasubandhu judges the tenth ground "receiving the dharma-king's office, like the crown prince attaining mastery among the king's sons, yet here there is a subtle obstacle of cognition, so there is no mastery; to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" (§2.4) — the tenth ground's position like the prince near the kingly position, yet with a last subtle obstacle of cognition, so the ultimate mastery not attained, and to counteract this obstacle the buddha-ground is spoken of separately. So "falling within the number of the buddhas" is not "being the ultimate buddha," like the anointed prince falling within the number of the kṣatriya kings yet not the reigning king. The tenth ground is the close of the causal stage, the brink of buddhahood; the severing of the subtle obstacle of cognition above it and the realizing of the buddha-fruit are spoken of separately. This is the full close of this axis's bodhisattva-path causal-stage course (the whole from BODHI-P01 onward), and the seam between the Realization-axis *Bodhi Path* and the Attainment-axis's buddha-fruit.

Taken together, the three layers — the last two grounds are the ripening of the bodhisattva's causal-stage function of benefiting others (the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom's unobstructed teaching) and substance of wisdom (the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground's great dharma-body with mastery complete) (3.1); the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground's consecration that confers office, falling within the number of the buddhas, fulfilling the ten powers, is the brink of buddhahood (3.2); yet Vasubandhu judges that above it a subtle obstacle of cognition remains and the buddha-ground is spoken of separately, so the tenth ground is the close of the causal stage and not the ultimate buddha-fruit (3.3) — jointly establish the thesis: **the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom (the perfection of power · benefiting others by unobstructed teaching) and the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground (the perfection of wisdom · the great dharma-body with mastery complete) are the ripening of the close of the bodhisattva's causal stage; the tenth ground's consecration that confers office, falling within the number of the buddhas, though near to buddhahood, has yet a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining and the buddha-ground spoken of separately — so the tenth ground is the close of the causal stage, "near buddhahood yet not yet buddhahood," and the close of the Realization-axis *Bodhi Path*, its buddha-ground above seaming to the Attainment-axis's final division.**

## IV. Interdisciplinary Convergence

(This paper deliberately omits the section of interdisciplinary convergence; the reason and the declaration of the omission are given in Section VI. In brief: the spine of this paper is the Mahāyāna noble-stage doctrine of "the ripening of benefiting-others and wisdom in the last two grounds, the consecration that confers office and falling within the number of the buddhas, yet a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining and the buddha-ground spoken of separately." Contemporary models of "the final stage of a certification," of "the transition of an heir's succession," of "the consummation of professional mastery" may, on the surface, be likened to "falling within the number of the buddhas yet a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining" in the one respect of "near consummation yet a last stage intervening," yet they cannot sustain a convergence free of forced analogy on the three meanings: that "the consecration that confers office is a realization anointed by the buddhas' water of wisdom, not the conferral of an institutional office"; that "falling within the number of the buddhas is a rank of obstacle-severing realization, not a nominal listing"; and that "the subtle obstacle of cognition is the last of the obstacles to be severed, not the last rung of a résumé." Above all, the meaning of "the buddhas' water of wisdom anointing the crown, fulfilling the ten powers, falling within the number of the buddhas yet a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining" marks the boundary of causal and fruit stages in obstacle-severing realization, which no social model of "institutional office-transfer / an heir's succession" can answer to: an heir's succession is the conferral of a title, the tenth ground's consecration the infusion of realization; the two are alike in face but other in spirit. Hence, per the series' editorial convention, the section is cut, and so declared here.)

## V. Middle-Way Corrections

This section clears away three misreadings: the first two concern the limits of the sources and later teaching-classifications for certain names, the third the boundary between the tenth ground and the buddha-ground — all demanding special honesty.

**⚠️ Correction 1: The relation of the Ground of Good Wisdom's "unobstructed power of teaching" to the later-matched "four unobstructed discernments (of dharma, meaning, expression, eloquence)" is not the express wording of this round's three sources, and is not supplied.** This must be marked first as a limit of the sources. This round's three sources — the *Eighty-fascicle Avataṃsaka* (T0279), the *Daśabhūmika-sūtra* (T0287), and Vasubandhu's *Daśabhūmika-vyākhyāna* (T1522) — at the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom read "by the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others, hence named the Ground of Good Wisdom" (§2.2), and judge it "the perfection of power predominant" (§2.2). What the three sources speak of is the layer "the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others, the perfection of power predominant"; they do not list the names of the "four unobstructed discernments (the unobstructed discernment of dharma, of meaning, of expression, of eloquence)." Later teaching-masters often match the "four unobstructed discernments" to the Ground of Good Wisdom's "unobstructed teaching"; this matching has its grounds, yet is not the express wording of this round's three sources. This paper, in its prose, follows the sources with "the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others, the perfection of power predominant," and does not presume to supply the names of the "four unobstructed discernments" into the textual evidence (their matching to await the author's adjudication by school and teaching). Should the reader meet the Ground of Good Wisdom glossed by the "four unobstructed discernments" elsewhere, let it be known as a later teaching-masters' matching, not the express word of this round's three sources.

**⚠️ Correction 2: "The awakening-of-equivalence and the wondrous awakening" as proper names of stages between the ten grounds and the buddha-ground are a later teaching-classification, not the express word of the three sources; further, the source's D-3 T0279 closing sentence, its line unverified, is neither cited nor fabricated by this paper.** Two things must be honestly marked. First, above the ten grounds and before the buddha-ground, later traditions (the *Yingluo-jing*, Tiantai, and the like) often set up the proper stage-names "the awakening-of-equivalence" and "the wondrous awakening." Yet this round's three sources, at the close of the ten grounds, read "the Dharma-Cloud Ground's consecration that confers office · falling within the number of the buddhas" (§2.3) and "yet a subtle obstacle of cognition · to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" (§2.4); they do not produce the proper names "the awakening-of-equivalence" or "the wondrous awakening." This paper, following the sources, marks the close of the ten grounds and the interval before the buddha-ground with "the consecration that confers office · falling within the number of the buddhas · the brink of buddhahood · yet a subtle obstacle of cognition · the buddha-ground spoken of separately," and does not presume to judge the proper stage-names "the awakening-of-equivalence, the wondrous awakening" (their proper names and the fine division between the ten grounds and the buddha-ground to await the author's adjudication by school and teaching). Second, the source unit holds besides a D-3 (an Avataṃsaka closing sentence, "the Dharma-Cloud Ground's office received … named abiding in the Dharma-Cloud Ground"), whose T0279 line was not verified at the gathering of evidence (the line found in the prior search proving to be a *Daśabhūmika-sūtra* T0287 line, not T0279); this paper, here choosing dearth over excess, neither cites nor fabricates the text of this closing sentence. Should this T0279 corresponding sentence later need to be supplied, the correct line must be re-verified at the *Eighty-fascicle Avataṃsaka*'s "Chapter on the Ten Grounds, part 6" (around and after 0206a).

**❌ Misreading 3: The tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground's consecration that confers office, "falling within the number of the buddhas," is the ultimate buddha-fruit, and the tenth-ground bodhisattva is a buddha.** This must be cleared and the boundary held — and this is also the last boundary of the whole *Bodhi Path* Realization-axis. "Falling within the number of the buddhas" and "fulfilling the ten powers" are indeed great aspects of nearness to the buddhas (a great advance over the eighth ground's "the buddhas' ten powers you have not yet attained"); yet Vasubandhu plainly judges that above the tenth ground "here there is a subtle obstacle of cognition, so there is no mastery; to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" (§2.4) — there is yet a last subtle obstacle of cognition unexhausted, so the buddha-ground is spoken of separately. "Falling within the number of the buddhas" is like the anointed prince's "falling within the number of the anointed kṣatriya kings" (the likeness of §2.3) — already bearing the king's qualifications, already listed in the number of the kings, yet the anointed prince is not yet the reigning king. To misread "falling within the number of the buddhas" as "being the ultimate buddha," and the tenth-ground bodhisattva as the same as the buddha-fruit, is to erase the last stage above the tenth ground of severing the subtle obstacle of cognition and realizing the buddha-ground, and to violate Vasubandhu's plain judgment "to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately." The tenth ground is the close of the causal stage, the brink of buddhahood, not the ultimate of the buddha-fruit; the severing of the subtle obstacle of cognition above it and the realizing of the tathāgata's mastery as the buddha-fruit are the affair spoken of separately by the Attainment-axis's final division (sambodhi), and the boundary and seam between it and the tenth ground are for the author to adjudicate — this paper marks only, from the text, "falling within the number of the buddhas yet a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining, the buddha-ground spoken of separately," and does not speak in the fruit-stage's stead.

## VI. Conclusion

**Essential restatement.** The ninth Ground of Good Wisdom and the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground are the close of the bodhisattva's causal stage — the last ripening of the "function of benefiting others" and the "substance of wisdom" — and the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground's consecration that confers office is the final stage of "near buddhahood yet not yet buddhahood." The ninth Ground of Good Wisdom has the "perfection of power" predominant and is named for "the unobstructed power of teaching, accomplishing the practice of benefiting others" (the ripening of the function of benefiting others); the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground has the "perfection of wisdom" predominant, the matching of the ten perfections here consummated, and is named for "attaining the great dharma-body, mastery complete" (the ripening of the substance of wisdom, the dharma-body like a cloud covering all). The tenth-ground bodhisattva enters the "position of conferred office (consecration)," the buddhas' water of wisdom anointing his crown, "because he fulfills the ten powers of the tathāgata, he falls within the number of the buddhas," likened to a wheel-turning sage-king anointing the crown of the crown prince with the water of the four great oceans to confer the kingly office. Yet the boundary must be held: though he falls within the number of the buddhas and fulfills the ten powers, Vasubandhu still judges that "here there is a subtle obstacle of cognition, so there is no mastery; to counteract this obstacle, the buddha-ground is spoken of separately" — above the tenth ground there is yet a last subtle obstacle of cognition, so the buddha-ground is spoken of separately. On the doctrine, three layers bear it: the last two grounds as the ripening of benefiting-others and wisdom (3.1); the consecration that confers office and falling within the number of the buddhas as the brink of buddhahood (3.2); the yet-remaining subtle obstacle of cognition and the buddha-ground spoken of separately as the close of the causal stage (3.3). It must be honestly marked: the Ground of Good Wisdom's "unobstructed teaching" and the later-matched "four unobstructed discernments" are not the express word of the three sources, and are not supplied; "the awakening-of-equivalence, the wondrous awakening" as proper names are a later teaching-classification, not the express word of the three sources, and this paper does not presume to judge them; the D-3 T0279 closing sentence, its line unverified, is neither included nor fabricated (Section V).

**Guidance for practice.** Though the last two grounds are noble-stage states at the close of the causal stage, beyond the ordinary being's reach, their doctrine offers the practitioner two things. First, to take from the sequence of "self-realization — benefiting others" a measure of clarity about order. The eighth ground's self-realization being firm, the ninth ground then broadens into the mastery of benefiting others by unobstructed teaching — this hints that truly powerful benefiting of others (unobstructed teaching) must be founded on the firmness of one's own realization; to teach others forcibly when one is not yet thorough easily errs both self and other. Yet one must not, holding to this, abandon the benefiting of others in the initial resolve: the bodhisattva's six perfections include from the start such other-benefiting practices as giving and discipline, to be practiced from the first arousing of the mind; what is said here of "founded on self-realization" refers specifically to "the mastery of unobstructed teaching" requiring realization as its base, not that benefiting others must await the consummation of self-realization. Self-benefit and benefiting others complete each other and do not wait upon each other; let this be well understood. Second, to take from "near buddhahood yet a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining" a measure of unself-satisfaction. Even at the near-buddhahood ground of the tenth ground's consecration that confers office, falling within the number of the buddhas, fulfilling the ten powers, Vasubandhu still judges a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining above it and the buddha-ground spoken of separately — how much more the practitioner? This decisively admonishes: on the path of practice, even at a most exalted ground there is yet an obstacle to be severed, a course to be advanced; the practitioner must by no means take a little for enough, take a momentary accord for the ultimate, or take a high mid-course position for the terminus. The near-buddhahood ground is not self-satisfied — how much less those not yet arrived?

**Open questions.** First, this paper deliberately cut the Section IV interdisciplinary convergence, for the reason given above. Second, the matching of the Ground of Good Wisdom's "unobstructed teaching" to the "four unobstructed discernments (of dharma, meaning, expression, eloquence)" is not the express wording of this round's three sources, and this paper does not supply it, its matching for the author to adjudicate. Third, "the awakening-of-equivalence, the wondrous awakening" as proper names of stages between the ten grounds and the buddha-ground are a later (Yingluo-jing / Tiantai) teaching-classification, not the express word of the three sources, and this paper does not presume to judge their proper stage-names, to await the author's adjudication by school and teaching. Fourth, the source's D-3 T0279 closing sentence, its line unverified, is neither included nor fabricated, to be supplied after the correct line is re-verified at the *Eighty-fascicle Avataṃsaka*'s "Chapter on the Ten Grounds, part 6." Fifth, the severing of the subtle obstacle of cognition above the tenth ground and the realizing of the tathāgata's mastery as the buddha-fruit are the affair spoken of separately by the Attainment-axis's final division (sambodhi); the boundary and seam between the tenth ground (the close of the causal stage) and the buddha-ground (the fruit stage) are for the author to adjudicate.

**The dual-axis seam (SEAM).** This paper is the close of the *Bodhi Path* Realization-axis (from the ten faiths to the ten grounds, BODHI-P01 to P10), and its seam is the most pivotal. This paper meets the Attainment-axis's final division at the two faces of the tenth ground's consecration that confers office: the Realization-axis (this paper) tells the "walking" of the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground — benefiting others by unobstructed teaching (the ninth Ground of Good Wisdom), attaining the great dharma-body with mastery complete (the tenth Dharma-Cloud Ground), the consecration that confers office and falling within the number of the buddhas (the close of the ten grounds); the Attainment-axis's final division then reads the fruit-virtue this accomplishes, and the buddha-fruit above it that severs the subtle obstacle of cognition (the tathāgata's mastery). It must above all be sharply distinguished: "falling within the number of the buddhas" is not "the ultimate buddha-fruit" — Vasubandhu judges a subtle obstacle of cognition remaining above it and the buddha-ground spoken of separately; the bodhisattva of the consecration that confers office is like the anointed crown prince (falling within the number of the kṣatriya kings yet not the reigning king), not the reigning buddha. So the tenth ground's mastery and the tathāgata's mastery have their boundary at this last subtle obstacle of cognition: the tenth ground is the close of the causal stage, the brink of buddhahood; the buddha-ground is the fruit stage that severs this subtle obstacle. The doctrine of the seam (including the severing of the subtle obstacle of cognition, the fine division between the ten grounds and the buddha-ground, and the proper stage-names of the awakening-of-equivalence and the wondrous awakening) is for the author to adjudicate; this paper does not speak in the Attainment-axis's stead. Herewith the *Bodhi Path*, from the first faith of the ten faiths, the suppression of afflictions of the three worthy ranks, the path of seeing of the first ground, the path of cultivation of the second to seventh grounds, the effortlessness of the eighth ground, the benefiting of others of the ninth ground, to the consecration that confers office of the tenth ground, comes to the full close of its causal-stage course; the buddha-fruit above it awaits the separate exposition of the Attainment-axis's final division.
