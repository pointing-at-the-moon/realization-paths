# The Ten Abodes, to the Consecration

**Entering the Buddha-Family as a Son of the Buddha — from the Abode of Arousing the Mind to the Abode of Consecration**

*中文主題：十住至灌頂住——入佛家為佛子，從發心到受職*

## Abstract

The preceding paper, BODHI-P03, set "entering the abodes" as the threshold — faith becoming dwelling, the beginning of position-non-regress. This paper walks the full course within the door: the Ten Abodes, from the first "Abode of Arousing the Mind" to the final "Abode of Consecration," form one complete arc of "entering the Buddha-family, maturing, and being invested as a son of the Buddha." In the *Avataṃsaka*'s Chapter on the Ten Abodes, the bodhisattva Dharma-wisdom lists the full names and number of the ten abodes (Arousing the Mind, Governing the Ground, Cultivation, Noble Birth, Endowment with Means, Right Mind, Non-Regress, Youthful Truth, the Prince, Consecration); the chapter opens by setting all ten as "dwelling in the family of the buddhas of the three times," "equal to the dharma-realm and space," and addresses their dwellers as "sons of the Buddha"; and the bodhisattva of the final "Abode of Consecration" "accomplishes ten kinds of knowledge" (shaking, illuminating, sustaining countless worlds, and the rest), whose bodily deeds, spiritual transformations, and knowledge of the three times are such that "even the Prince-of-the-Dharma bodhisattva cannot know them." This paper argues: the very names of the Ten Abodes disclose an arc of royal lineage — Noble Birth (born noble within the Buddha-family), Youthful Truth, the Prince (of the Dharma), Consecration (as a prince is anointed to inherit the throne); within the family of the buddhas of the three times the bodhisattva, from the entry of arousing the mind, matures by degrees, and at the Abode of Consecration is invested as a true Dharma-prince, accomplishing the ten kinds of knowledge. The paper reads this arc from the canonical names (the full list) and the framing (Buddha-family · son of the Buddha), and details the mark of the final consecration; the per-abode doctrine of the middle abodes, for want of assembled treatise-texts (see Section V), is deliberately not unfolded one by one. A strict bound: though the Abode of Consecration is the consummation of the last of the Ten Abodes, it remains a causal-stage position, still the first of the three worthy ranks, not buddhahood — the long road of the Ten Practices, Ten Dedications, and Ten Grounds lies above. The conclusion: the Ten Abodes are the maturing of a son of the Buddha within the Buddha-family, from entering the house to being invested, completing the "abode-position," the first stretch of the worthy ranks.

**Keywords**: Ten Abodes, Abode of Consecration, entering the Buddha-family, son of the Buddha, Dharma-prince, accomplishing ten kinds of knowledge, Abode of Noble Birth, Abode of Youthful Truth, three worthy ranks, Avataṃsaka Ten Abodes chapter, causal stage, investiture and succession

## I. The Question

The last paper carried the practitioner through the threshold: faith becomes dwelling, and the Abode of Arousing the Mind is where position-non-regress begins (BODHI-P03). But "entering the abodes" is only the first step through the door — within the door is a whole hall of chambers. The Ten Abodes are not a point but ten successive "abodes," from the first "Abode of Arousing the Mind," through Governing the Ground, Cultivation, Noble Birth, Endowment with Means, Right Mind, Non-Regress, Youthful Truth, the Prince, to the final "Abode of Consecration." Once through the door, how are these ten chambers walked? And reaching the tenth, the "Abode of Consecration," what has the bodhisattva become?

The canon gives these ten abodes one common place and one common standing: they all "dwell in the family of the buddhas of the three times," and all who dwell in them are called "sons of the Buddha" (cited in BODHI-P03). And once the names of the ten are read in sequence, an arc emerges — an arc of royal lineage: born noble within the Buddha-family (Noble Birth), a "youthful" child of the royal house (Youthful Truth), grown into the "Prince [of the Dharma]" (the Prince), and at last receiving "consecration" to inherit the throne (Consecration). Consecration (*abhiṣeka*) was in India the great investiture for establishing a crown prince or enthroning a king — pouring the waters of the seas over the prince's crown, that he might thereafter succeed to the throne and act as king. That the Ten Abodes close with the "Abode of Consecration" says just this: within the Buddha-family the bodhisattva, from the entry of arousing the mind, matures all the way, and here is invested as a true son of the Dharma-King.

The thesis of this paper: **the Ten Abodes form one complete arc of "entering the Buddha-family as a son of the Buddha" — from entering the house at the Abode of Arousing the Mind, to investiture and succession at the Abode of Consecration, accomplishing the ten kinds of knowledge; within the family of the buddhas of the three times the bodhisattva matures from birth to investiture, completing the "abode-position," the first stretch of the three worthy ranks.** This takes the baton from BODHI-P03: P03 set the threshold of "entering the abodes" and the sense of "position-non-regress"; this paper walks the ten chambers within the door, all the way to the consecration of investiture.

One boundary of this paper's sources must be stated honestly up front, and Section V will return to it. The textual evidence this paper rests on is the **full list** of the ten names, the framing of the ten as set within the **Buddha-family** and addressed as **sons of the Buddha**, and the mark of the final **Abode of Consecration accomplishing ten kinds of knowledge**; the *per-abode* doctrinal definitions of the middle abodes (Governing the Ground, Cultivation, Noble Birth, Endowment with Means, Right Mind, Non-Regress, Youthful Truth, the Prince) — the treatise-texts (the exegeses of Fazang and Chengguan, and the like) — are not yet assembled in this round's source-gathering. So this paper reads the **arc** and the **final abode** from "names plus framing," and is not a rung-by-rung exposition; the one-by-one unfolding of the middle eight awaits a later round of source-gathering and a separate paper. This is "reading the momentum of the whole course," not "expounding the detail of every stage."

The argument proceeds in three moves. Section II lays out the canonical basis: the full list of the ten; the Buddha-family / son-of-the-Buddha framing; the ten knowledges of the Abode of Consecration. Section III is the core argument, in three layers: the Ten Abodes as an "entering the Buddha-family" arc of maturation; the sense of consecration as "investiture and succession"; and the strict bound that "even at the Abode of Consecration it is still a causal-stage position." Section IV deliberately omits interdisciplinary convergence, for the reason in Section VI. Section V offers middle-way corrections, marking honestly the "not unfolded per-abode" boundary and the "names set side by side" principle. Section VI concludes, with the cross-reference index, and looks ahead to P05 (the Ten Practices) and the Attainment-axis seam.

## II. The Canonical Basis

### 2.1 The full list of the ten abodes: from Arousing the Mind to Consecration

In the *Avataṃsaka*'s Chapter on the Ten Abodes, the bodhisattva Dharma-wisdom lists the full names and number of the bodhisattva's ten abodes:

> 菩薩住有十種，過去、未來、現在諸佛，已說、當說、今說。何者為十？所謂：初發心住、治地住、修行住、生貴住、具足方便住、正心住、不退住、童真住、王子住、灌頂住。是名菩薩十住，去、來、現在諸佛所說。[^1]
>
> *"The bodhisattva's abodes are of ten kinds, which the buddhas of past, future, and present have taught, will teach, now teach. What are the ten? The Abode of Arousing the Mind, of Governing the Ground, of Cultivation, of Noble Birth, of Endowment with Means, of Right Mind, of Non-Regress, of Youthful Truth, of the Prince [of the Dharma], of Consecration. These are called the bodhisattva's ten abodes, taught by the buddhas of the past, future, and present."*

The ten in order: Arousing the Mind, Governing the Ground, Cultivation, Noble Birth, Endowment with Means, Right Mind, Non-Regress, Youthful Truth, the Prince, Consecration. Set the names of the last four side by side — Noble Birth, Youthful Truth, the Prince, Consecration — and a thread already forms within the names: "born into a noble house → a youthful child → the (Dharma-)Prince → receiving consecration." Section III will follow this thread to read out the maturation-arc of the Ten Abodes. (Note: the fifth here reads "Endowment with Means" (具足方便住), a re-ordering of "Means-Endowment" (方便具足住); the ninth reads "the Prince" (王子住), an abbreviation of "Prince-of-the-Dharma" (法王子住), and the passage on the Abode of Consecration below likewise reads "the Prince." Both are the sutra's own internal wordings, set side by side without taking a side; see Section V.)

### 2.2 The Buddha-family / son-of-the-Buddha framing: the ten all dwell in the family of the buddhas of the three times

Why can these ten abodes be strung into a single line of "maturing within the Buddha-family"? Because they share one place and one standing. The Chapter on the Ten Abodes opens — Dharma-wisdom, emerging from concentration, addresses the assembly:

> 佛子！菩薩住處廣大，與法界虛空等。佛子！菩薩住三世諸佛家，彼菩薩住，我今當說。[^2]
>
> *"O son of the Buddha, the bodhisattva's dwelling-place is vast, equal to the dharma-realm and to space. O son of the Buddha, the bodhisattva dwells in the family of the buddhas of the three times; that bodhisattva's dwelling I shall now expound."*

"The bodhisattva dwells in the family of the buddhas of the three times" — this line is the framing of the ten abodes: not one of the ten "abodes" expounded is anything but a "dwelling in the family of the buddhas of the three times." A family is a place of upbringing; dwelling in one family, the ten abodes are not ten isolated points but the ten successive stages by which one and the same "child of the house" enters, matures, and comes to fullness within that house. And just because they dwell in one Buddha-family, the whole chapter addresses all who abide in the ten as "sons of the Buddha" (argued in BODHI-P03, 2.2). "Buddha-family" is the place; "son of the Buddha" is the standing; together they give the ten abodes a unified reading: the maturing of a son of the Buddha within the Buddha-family.

### 2.3 The final Abode of Consecration: accomplishing ten knowledges, unknowable even to the Dharma-prince

The terminus of this maturation-arc — the tenth, the "Abode of Consecration" — the Chapter on the Ten Abodes describes thus:

> 佛子！云何為菩薩灌頂住？此菩薩得成就十種智。何者為十？所謂：震動無數世界、照耀無數世界……令無數眾生調伏。是為十。佛子！此菩薩身及身業，神通變現，過去智、未來智、現在智成就佛土，心境界、智境界皆不可知，乃至法王子菩薩亦不能知。[^3]
>
> *"O son of the Buddha, what is the bodhisattva's Abode of Consecration? This bodhisattva accomplishes ten kinds of knowledge. What are the ten? Shaking countless worlds, illuminating countless worlds, … bringing countless beings to be tamed. These are the ten. O son of the Buddha, this bodhisattva's body and bodily deeds, spiritual penetrations and transformations, and the knowledge of past, future, and present that perfects the buddha-lands — the realm of his mind and the realm of his knowledge cannot be known, cannot be known even by the Prince-of-the-Dharma bodhisattva."*

Two points are crucial. First, the bodhisattva of the Abode of Consecration "accomplishes ten kinds of knowledge," whose function is "shaking countless worlds, illuminating countless worlds, … bringing countless beings to be tamed" — already a vast and inconceivable capacity. Second, and showing the depth of the position still more: this bodhisattva's bodily deeds, spiritual transformations, and the three-times knowledge that perfects buddha-lands — the "realm of his mind and the realm of his knowledge cannot be known, cannot be known even by the Prince-of-the-Dharma bodhisattva." Even the preceding position (the ninth, the "Prince," the Dharma-prince bodhisattva) cannot fathom the realm of the tenth — this gap of "the later position deeper than the earlier, and so deep the earlier cannot know it" marks precisely the weight of the "Abode of Consecration" as the terminus of the ten abodes, the investiture-and-succession. As, after a crown prince receives consecration, his standing is beyond comparison with the princes who have not.

## III. The Core Argument

Section II presented three things: the full list of the ten (the last four names carrying a royal thread); the ten dwelling in one Buddha-family and addressed as sons of the Buddha (a unifying frame); the Abode of Consecration accomplishing ten knowledges, unknowable even to the Dharma-prince (the depth of the terminus). This section, in three layers: reading the maturation-arc of the Ten Abodes from the names and the frame (3.1); the sense of consecration as "investiture and succession" (3.2); the strict bound that "even at the Abode of Consecration it is still a causal-stage position" (3.3).

### 3.1 The Ten Abodes as an "entering the Buddha-family" arc — read from the names and the frame

Set the "Buddha-family · son of the Buddha" frame (2.2) beside the names of the ten (2.1), and the ten are no longer ten parallel items but the successive degrees by which a "child of the house" enters and comes to fullness. This arc is carved most clearly into the names of the last four: **Noble Birth** (born into the Buddha-family, the lineage already noble), **Youthful Truth** (like the youthful child of a royal house, pure and unmixed), **the Prince** (grown into a son of the Dharma-King), **Consecration** (receiving the rite of consecration to inherit the throne). Birth — youth — prince — consecration: this is plainly a line of "royal lineage from nativity to coronation." And the earlier six (the entering-the-house first mind of Arousing the Mind, the grounding of the mind in Governing the Ground, the taking-up of practice in Cultivation, Endowment with Means, Right Mind, the firmed mind-ground of Non-Regress), as the earlier segment of this line, though this paper does not gloss them rung by rung (see Section V), can in their overall momentum likewise be read from the frame: all are stages by which this child of the Buddha-family, having entered the house, is by degrees nurtured and grown within it.

The boundary of this reading must be stated with care: this paper reads the "momentum" of this arc from the **canonical names** (A1, the full list, verbatim at 2.1) and the **canonical frame** (A2, Buddha-family and son of the Buddha, 2.2), and does *not* supply for the middle abodes their respective per-abode doctrines. The royal imagery of "Noble Birth — Youthful Truth — Prince — Consecration" within the names is the sutra's own (not this paper's invention); to follow it and read out a maturation-arc is a structural, restrained reading. As for the inner-realization state of each abode, the detail of what it severs and cultivates — the treatise-texts are not assembled, and this paper does not supply them in their stead (Section V details this).

A contrast: reading a table of contents that lists only the chapter titles is a different thing from closely reading the whole book chapter by chapter — yet the former is not without yield. When the titles of a table of contents are themselves arranged as "birth — christening — coming-of-age — inheriting the title," then even without reading the body of each chapter you may justly say: "this is the biography of someone's growing up within a noble house and at last inheriting." The names of the Ten Abodes are just such a table of contents whose titles already disclose the arc; what this paper reads is that arc, not the body of each chapter.

> ✅ Doctrinally sound: the ten abodes all dwell in "the family of the buddhas of the three times," their dwellers all called "sons of the Buddha" (A2), while the last four names "Noble Birth — Youthful Truth — Prince — Consecration" (A1) are the sutra's own royal imagery; to read from these names and this frame the arc of "a son of the Buddha maturing within the Buddha-family from entry to fullness" is a structural reading grounded in the canonical text, not a per-abode supplement.

### 3.2 The sense of consecration: investiture and succession, accomplishing ten knowledges

The terminus of the arc is the "Abode of Consecration," and the two words "consecration" themselves carry this arc's sense of completion. Consecration (*abhiṣeka*) was in India the great investiture for establishing a crown prince or enthroning a king — pouring the waters of the four great seas over the prince's crown, that he might thereafter succeed to the throne and be fit to act as king. That the Ten Abodes close with the "Abode of Consecration" borrows just this image: after the bodhisattva has matured into a Dharma-prince within the Buddha-family, he receives "consecration" to succeed to the Dharma-King's place and be fit to do the Dharma-King's work. It is the graduation, the investiture, of this stretch of "abode-position" nurturing.

What is the mark of having been invested? The sutra says the bodhisattva of the Abode of Consecration "accomplishes ten kinds of knowledge," able to shake, to illuminate, to sustain countless worlds, and even to bring countless beings to be tamed (2.3). These "ten knowledges" are not abstract titles but the real capacities by which the invested one is fit for his work — as a new king, once consecrated, holds the powers to command the four quarters. Showing the loftiness of the position still more is the gap "cannot be known even by the Prince-of-the-Dharma bodhisattva": the Dharma-prince of the preceding position cannot fathom the realm of mind and knowledge of this consecrated one. Between the invested and the not-yet-invested stands a door the earlier position cannot cross. This is why the "Abode of Consecration" is fit to cap the ten abodes, the crown of the first of the three worthy ranks.

A contrast: the crown prince versus the crowned king. The Abode of the Prince is like the prince already established as heir — his standing noble, his title fixed, yet not yet enthroned. The Abode of Consecration is like the king who has gone through the rite of coronation and is formally enthroned — the reach of his command thereafter (the function of the ten knowledges) is beyond what the heir-period could reach; hence "the Dharma-prince cannot know." Both in the royal house, both a "son," yet the rite of coronation marks a leap in real capacity and in standing. That the Ten Abodes close with consecration marks just this leap.

> ✅ Doctrinally sound: the "Abode of Consecration" is named for the Indian rite of consecration-and-succession; its mark is "accomplishing ten knowledges" (shaking · illuminating · sustaining countless worlds, A4), and its realm "cannot be known even by the Prince-of-the-Dharma bodhisattva"; this shows the Abode of Consecration to be the investiture-and-succession of the last of the ten abodes, the consummation of the abode-position's nurturing.

### 3.3 The strict bound: "even at the Abode of Consecration" it is still a causal-stage position, not buddhahood

The one misreading most to be guarded against is to be dazzled by the words "consecration," "accomplishing ten knowledges," "even the Dharma-prince cannot know," and to suppose that at the Abode of Consecration one has already become a buddha, already reached the ultimate. It must be drawn clear: **though the Abode of Consecration is the consummation of the last of the ten abodes, it is still a causal-stage position, still the first of the three worthy ranks (the abode-position), not buddhahood.**

Three reasons. First, by position: the Abode of Consecration is the last of the Ten Abodes (the second ten of the fifty positions); above it lie still the Ten Practices, Ten Dedications, and Ten Grounds (the fifty-position map of the PLAN), a long road — the three worthy ranks (abodes · practices · dedications) have but finished the first segment of abodes, and the path of seeing and path of cultivation of the ten noble grounds have not yet begun. Second, by terminology: "consecration" here is "the Dharma-*prince* receiving consecration" — what is succeeded to is a "son of the Dharma-King's" investiture within the causal stage, the same in name as but other in position from the ultimate of the Tathāgata's fruit-ground; only the later segment of this axis (the ten grounds, and beyond them equal- and wondrous-enlightenment, the latter belonging to the third division `sambodhi`) approaches the fruit-ground, and this division (the Bodhi Path) ends at the Cloud-of-Dharma ground, the last of the ten grounds. Third, the line "cannot be known even by the Prince-of-the-Dharma bodhisattva" shows the *positional gap* of "the later abode deeper than the earlier," and that very fact says it remains within the comparison of "abode" with "abode" — a height-and-depth internal to the Ten Abodes, not a having-left-the-ten-abodes-into-buddhahood.

So the "knowledge" of "accomplishing ten knowledges" is the knowledge of the abode-position, the capacity of the causal stage, not to be straightway equated with the omniscient knowledge of the Tathāgata's fruit-ground. To read the Abode of Consecration as buddhahood is to mistake the "investiture" of the nurturing-period for the "accomplishment of the Way" of the finale — as one might mistake a prince's coronation for the end of the dynasty. Coronation is the beginning, not the end, of a sovereign's career; the Abode of Consecration is the consummation of the bodhisattva's "abode-position" nurturing, after which he is precisely to go out and benefit others (the Ten Practices), dedicate (the Ten Dedications), and enter the grounds (the Ten Grounds) — the long road just then opening.

> ⚠️ Heuristic analogy / strict bound: "the Abode of Consecration is buddhahood" is a misreading to be cleared away. The Abode of Consecration is the consummation of the last of the ten abodes, the first of the three worthy ranks, still a causal-stage position; its "ten knowledges" are the knowledge of the abode-position, not the Tathāgata's omniscient knowledge; "even the Dharma-prince cannot know" shows the positional gap of the later abode over the earlier, still within the Ten Abodes. The figure of a sovereign's "coronation as beginning, not end" takes only the one sense "investiture ≠ finale," and does not assert any doctrinal equation of the bodhisattva position with kingship.

Taken together, the three layers — the Ten Abodes form an arc of "entering the Buddha-family and maturing" read from the names (Noble Birth — Youthful Truth — Prince — Consecration) and the frame (Buddha-family · son of the Buddha) (3.1); the Abode of Consecration, borrowing the rite of consecration-and-succession, shows by "ten knowledges · the Dharma-prince cannot know" the investiture-consummation of the abode-position's nurturing (3.2); yet this consummation is still a causal-stage position, the first of the three worthy ranks, not buddhahood, its knowledge the knowledge of the abode-position (3.3) — jointly establish the thesis: **the Ten Abodes are the nurturing-period of a son of the Buddha within the Buddha-family, from entering the house (Arousing the Mind) to investiture (Consecration), completing the "abode-position," the first stretch of the worthy ranks — a consummation of the causal stage, not the ultimate of the fruit-ground.**

## V. Middle-Way Corrections

This section clears away three misreadings; the first two bear on the boundary of this paper's sources, and must be marked honestly.

**⚠️ Correction 1: the per-abode doctrine of the middle abodes (Governing the Ground, Cultivation, Noble Birth, Endowment with Means, Right Mind, Non-Regress, Youthful Truth, the Prince) is not unfolded one by one in this paper.** This is the clear boundary of this paper's sources. The "maturation-arc" it sets out is the *structural* momentum read from the ten abodes' **canonical names** (A1, the full list) and the **Buddha-family · son-of-the-Buddha frame** (A2); it does *not* supply for the middle eight their respective per-abode inner-realization states, what they sever and cultivate. Why not? Because the definitional pivots of the abodes' positions require treatise-texts (the *Avataṃsaka* exegeses of Fazang and Chengguan, say, on the stational classification of each abode), and such texts are not yet assembled in this round's source-gathering. This paper keeps the precept "do not supply per-abode doctrine not yet collated through source-gathering" — reading what the names plainly show (the momentum of the arc, the mark of the final abode), without making good for the canon what has not yet been assembled (the detail of each abode). The per-abode unfolding of the middle eight awaits a later round of source-gathering (assembling the treatise-texts) and a separate paper. Let the reader not take this paper's "reading the arc" as a substitute for "expounding the rungs."

**⚠️ Correction 2: the variant names are set side by side, with no side taken.** Within the names of the ten abodes the sutra itself has variant wordings: the fifth reads "Endowment with Means" (具足方便住), a re-ordering of "Means-Endowment" (方便具足住); the ninth reads "the Prince" (王子住), an abbreviation of "Prince-of-the-Dharma" (法王子住) (the passage on the Abode of Consecration likewise reads "the Prince"). This paper throughout follows the sutra's own wording (80-fascicle *Avataṃsaka*, T0279) and sets them side by side, without selecting a "standard form" between "Endowment-with-Means / Means-Endowment" or "Prince / Prince-of-the-Dharma." Should a reader meet, elsewhere, one form taken as the fixed name, let it be known that this is that source's selection.

**❌ Misreading 3: "the Abode of Consecration" = buddhahood, the ultimate fruit.** The name of consecration, the function of the ten knowledges, the depth of "even the Dharma-prince cannot know" — all easily lead one to misjudge the Abode of Consecration as having reached buddhahood. It must be cleared: the Abode of Consecration is the consummation of the last of the ten abodes, the first of the three worthy ranks (the abode-position), still a **causal-stage** position. Above it lie still the long road of the Ten Practices, Ten Dedications, and Ten Grounds (the fifty positions of the PLAN); its "accomplishing ten knowledges" is the knowledge of the abode-position, the capacity of the causal stage, not the Tathāgata's omniscient knowledge of the fruit-ground; "even the Dharma-prince cannot know" shows the *positional gap* of "the later abode deeper than the earlier," which says it remains within the height-and-depth internal to the Ten Abodes, not having left them into buddhahood. This division (the Bodhi Path) ends at the Cloud-of-Dharma ground, the last of the ten grounds; the equal- and wondrous-enlightenment that approach the fruit-ground belong separately to the third division `sambodhi`. The figure of "a prince's coronation" takes only the one sense "investiture is the beginning of a career, not its finale"; let one not, on the strength of the name "consecration," misjudge that the Buddha-way is already accomplished.

## VI. Conclusion

**Essential restatement.** The Ten Abodes form one complete arc of nurturing as "entering the Buddha-family as a son of the Buddha," from entering the house at the Abode of Arousing the Mind to investiture-and-succession at the Abode of Consecration. On the textual side, the *Avataṃsaka*'s Chapter on the Ten Abodes lists the full names and number of the ten (A1), opens by setting all ten as "dwelling in the family of the buddhas of the three times · equal to the dharma-realm and space," addressing their dwellers as "sons of the Buddha" (A2), and at the final Abode of Consecration has the bodhisattva "accomplish ten knowledges" such that "even the Prince-of-the-Dharma bodhisattva cannot know them" (A4). On the doctrinal side, three layers support it: from the names (Noble Birth — Youthful Truth — Prince — Consecration) and the frame (Buddha-family · son of the Buddha) is read the arc of "a son of the Buddha maturing within the Buddha-family from entry to fullness" (3.1); the Abode of Consecration, borrowing the rite of consecration-and-succession, shows by "ten knowledges · the Dharma-prince cannot know" the investiture-consummation of the abode-position's nurturing (3.2); yet this consummation is still a causal-stage position, the first of the three worthy ranks, its knowledge the knowledge of the abode-position, not buddhahood (3.3). The paper keeps the precept of its sources: it reads what the names and the final abode plainly show, and does not supply the middle eight's per-abode doctrine, not yet assembled through source-gathering (Section V).

**Guidance for practice.** "Entering the Buddha-family as a son of the Buddha" is no abstract title on paper but a real nurturing — as a prince, within the house, from nativity, through youth, grows up and is invested. For the practitioner who has crossed the threshold of "position-non-regress" yet still feels himself unremarkable, this paper is a placement: the Ten Abodes are precisely the period of being nurtured within the Buddha-family after the standing (son of the Buddha) has been made good — you need not rush to display some marvel; the work of this stretch is to grow up, solidly, within the "house." And the mark of the Abode of Consecration's "accomplishing ten knowledges" reminds the practitioner: the consummation of the abode-position points toward the real capacity "fit to do the Dharma-King's work" and the power to benefit others, not toward a capping of the self; to be invested is precisely to go out and take up the work — which leads on to the next stretch (the Ten Practices) of benefiting others.

**Open questions.** First, this paper deliberately cut the Section IV interdisciplinary convergence, for the reason above. Second, the gap most to be filled: the per-abode doctrine of the middle eight (each abode's inner realization, what it severs and cultivates, its stational classification) — for want of assembled treatise-texts in this round's source-gathering, this paper has read only the arc and not unfolded the rungs; once the exegeses of Fazang, Chengguan, and the like are assembled, a separate paper should supply the per-abode detail of the ten abodes. Third, the abode-position is consummated at consecration, and its pointing is the real capacity to benefit others, "fit to do the Dharma-King's work"; so the bodhisattva is now to go out the door of the Buddha-family and act for others' benefit — the six perfections as conduct, taken up by the Ten Practices. BODHI-P05 will treat the Ten Practices (the practice-position): the benefiting of others and the enactment of the six perfections, turning from "maturing within the house" to "going out to benefit others."

**The seam (SEAM).** This paper meets the Attainment-axis `attainment-signatures/bodhisattva` at the "ten knowledges of the Abode of Consecration": the Realization axis (this paper) says these ten knowledges are "the investiture-consummation of the abode-position's nurturing" — a matter, in the journey, of "reaching the summit of the abode-position"; the Attainment axis reads the fruit-virtue these ten knowledges sign (one portion of bodhisattva mastery accrued in the abode-position stretch). It must be strictly distinguished from the Attainment-axis `tathagata`'s "ten masteries of the Tathāgata" (the fruit-position): the ten knowledges of the Abode of Consecration are the knowledge of the causal stage, the abode-position, not the masteries of the Tathāgata's fruit-ground (PLAN: the eighth ground's ten masteries are likewise causal-stage, distinct from the Tathāgata's ten masteries; the Abode of Consecration's ten knowledges are causal-stage by the same token). The doctrine of the seam is the author's to ratify; this paper does not speak for the Attainment axis.
